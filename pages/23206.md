## Idea

An [[orbifold]] is called *presentable* if it is ([[Morita equivalence|Morita]]-)[[equivalence in an (infinity,1)-category|equivalently]] a [[global quotient orbifold]] of a [[smooth manifold]] by the [[group action|action]] of some  [[Lie group]] (sometimes assumed/required to be [[compact Lie group|compact]]).

For example, (very) [[good orbifolds]] are presentable, since ([[finite group|finite]]) [[discrete groups]] are examples of ([[compact Lie group|compact]]) [[Lie groups]].

More interestingly, every [[effective orbifold]] $\mathcal{X}$ is presentable, namely as the [[global quotient orbifold|quotient]] of its [[frame bundle]] by the [[general linear group]], or equivalently of its orthonormal frame bundle (with respect to some [[Riemannian structure]]) by the [[orthogonal group]]: $\mathcal{X} \,\simeq\, Fr_{orth}(\mathcal{X}) \sslash O(dim(\mathcal{X}))$ ([Satake 1956](#Satake56),  recalled in, e.g., [Henriques & Metzler 2004, Prop. 1.4](#HenriquesMetzler04)).

In fact, it is [[conjecture|conjectured]] that *every* orbifold is presentable as the [[global quotient orbifold|global quotient]] of a smooth manifold by some Lie group ([Adem, Leida & Ruan 2007, Conj. 1.55](#AdemLeidaRuan07)).
Some results in this direction are presented in [Henriques & Metzler 2004, Sec. 5](#HenriquesMetzler04)
