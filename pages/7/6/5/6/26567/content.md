## Definition

\begin{definition}
\label{TheDefinition}
Let $M,N$ be a [[pair]] of [[group|groups]] (not necessarily [[abelian group|abelian]]) endowed with [[group action|actions]]  by [[group homomorphism|group]] [[automorphisms]] on each other: $\rho$ an action of $M$ on $N$, and $\sigma$ of $N$ on $M$. 

Then the *tensor product* $M \otimes N$ is the group generated by [[elements]] of the form $m\otimes n$ subject to the following [[generators and relations|relations]]:

* $(m m')\otimes n 
  \,=\,   
  (m m' m^{-1} \otimes \rho(m)(n))(m\otimes n),$

* $m\otimes (n n') \,=\, (m\otimes n)(\sigma(n)(m)\otimes n n' n^{-1} ),$

for $m,m'\in M$ and $n,n'\in N$.
\end{definition}

This definition is due to [Brown & Loday 1987, Section 2](#BL87) (there in the context of the [[van Kampen theorem]]).

+-- {: .num_remark}
###### Remark
Def \ref{TheDefinition} is to be understood as the generalization of the [[tensor product of abelian groups]], since as one can verify, whenever $M,N$ act trivially on each other, then the above definition reduces to the ordinary [[tensor product of abelian groups|tensor product]] of the [[abelianizations]] $(-)^{op}$ of the given groups:

$$
M\otimes N\cong M^{ab} \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} N^{ab}.
$$
=--
