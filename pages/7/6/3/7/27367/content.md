## Idea

In ([[differential topology|differential]]) [[topology]] and [[differential geometry]], a *punctured* [[manifold]] is the [[complement]] $M \setminus S$ of a [[finite set]] $S \subset M$ of points of a given [[manifold]] $M$.

This is most prominent in the case where $M \equiv \Sigma^b_g$ is a [[surface]] of [[genus of a surface|genus]] $g \in \mathbb{N}$ with $b \in \mathbb{N}$ [[boundary of a manifold|boundary]] [[connected components|components]]: For $\mathbf{n} = \{s_1, \cdots, s_n\} \subset \Sigma^b_g$ any [[finite set|finite]] [[subset]] of [[interior points]], there is the *punctured surface* $\Sigma^b_{g,n} \,\simeq\, \Sigma^b_{g,n}  \setminus \mathbf{n}$.

Accordingly one speaks of *punctured [[disks]]*, *punctured [[spheres]]*, etc.
