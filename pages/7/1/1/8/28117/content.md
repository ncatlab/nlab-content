## Idea

Given a system of finitely many word equations 

$$
F_\alpha(a_1,\ldots,a_n,x_1,\ldots,x_m) = 1
$$
where $\alpha = 1,\ldots, K$,
with coefficients $a_1,\ldots,a_n$ in a group $G$, a solutions in $G$ for unknowns $x_1,\ldots,x_m$ supplies the evaluation "functional" $Hom(G_F,G)$, where $G_F$ is
the product of $n+m$ copies of $G$ modulo the normal subgroup supplied by all $F_\alpha$ and fixation of constants $a_i$. In 1982, Makanin found an algorithm which decides for a finite system of equations over a free group on $s$ letters has a solution and produces all solutions when at least one solution exists. Razborov developed the theory further in his thesis. Later similar schemes called Makanin-Razborov diagrams for finding $Hom(H,G)$ in larger generality have been introduced by various authors, including Sela, Reinfeldt, Weidman, Bestvina, Feighn where $H$ is finitely generated groupm (and also for some classes of semigroups, free associative algebras etc.) and $F$ being a free group or a hyperbolic group; they also considered similar constructions for some other classes of algebras like semigroups and free associative algebras.
