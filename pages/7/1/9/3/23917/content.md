## Idea

### In solid state physics

In [[solid state physics]], the *Heisenberg model*
is  [[spin chain]]-[[model (in theoretical physics)|model]] for [[magnetism|magnetic]] [[quantum materials]]. 

The Heisenberg model is akin to the [[Ising model]], but where the latter only has [[internal symmetry]]-group [[cyclic group of order 2|$\mathbb{Z}/2$]] (spin flips along one axis), the Heisenberg model has the full [[Spin(3)]] $\simeq$ [[SU(2)]]-group of [[internal symmetries]] [[group action|acting]] on [[spin representations]] located at each of the "sites" of the model.

There are generalizations of the model with [[SU(N)|$SU(N)$]]-[[internal symmetry]] for any $N \in \mathbb{N}$.

### In SYM theory and AdS/CFT

As an abstract [[model (in theoretical physics)|model]], the Heisenberg spin chain has a curious re-incarnation as describing the [[dynamics]] of [[BMN operators]] in [[D=4 N=4 super Yang-Mills theory]] under the [[dilatation operator]] in the [[large-N limit]] of the [[AdS/CFT correspondence]]. See [here](single+trace+operator#ReferencesRelationToStringExcitations) for more.

## Properties

### Symmetry protected topological phase

The Heisenberg model has a curious [[internal symmetry|internal]]-[[symmetry protected topological phase]],  observed in [Haldane 1983](#Haldane83) and hence known as the *Haldane phase*.
