__Yuri Vladimirovi&#269; Matiyasevich__ (also spelled Jurij/Yurij/Yurii Matijasevi&#269;/Matijasevich/Matiasevich, Rus. &#1070;&#769;&#1088;&#1080;&#1081; &#1042;&#1083;&#1072;&#1076;&#1080;&#769;&#1084;&#1080;&#1088;&#1086;&#1074;&#1080;&#1095; &#1052;&#1072;&#1090;&#1080;&#1103;&#1089;&#1077;&#769;&#1074;&#1080;&#1095;) is a Russian
mathematician who obtained the negative solution to the Hilbert's tenth problem
By a seminal theorem of Matiyasevich, for every statement in mathematics (say ZF set theory) there is a Diophantine equation whose solvability is equivalent to the validity of the statement. This is not of much practical, but of large principal importance.
