## Idea

An _almost locally Euclidean space_ or _ALE space_ for short is a solution to the Euclidean [[Einstein equations]] which is a [[blow-up]] of an [[ADE-orbifold]] singularity $\mathbb{C}^2/\Gamma$ for [[finite group|finite]] [[subgroup]] $\Gamma \hookrightarrow SU(2)$.

* {#Kronheimer89} [[Peter Kronheimer]], _The construction of ALE spaces as hyper-K&#228;hler quotients_, J. Differential Geom. Volume 29, Number 3 (1989), 665-683. ([Euclid](https://projecteuclid.org/euclid.jdg/1214443066))

* {#IntriligatorSeiberg96} [[Ken Intriligator]], [[Nathan Seiberg]], _Mirror Symmetry in Three Dimensional Gauge Theories_ ([arXiv:hep-th/9607207](http://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/9607207))

this result is interpreted physically as describing the [[moduli space]] of [[vacua]] of [[gauge theories]] with [[spontaneously broken symmetry]] ("Higgs branches"). See at _[[3d mirror symmetry]]_ for more on this.
