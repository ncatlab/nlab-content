David E. Radford is an American algebraist, retired at the University of Illinois at Chicago. 

* [homepage](http://homepages.math.uic.edu/~radford)

An example of finite dimensional noncommutative [[Hopf algebra]]s is named after him (Radford's Hopf algebras) with most famous member in dimension 8. The purpose of his example originally was to  give an example of a Hopf algebra whose [[Jacobson radical]] is not a [[Hopf ideal]].
