(Solutions of) holonomic systems of differential equations are formalized in the notion of a holonomic D-module. A [[D-module]] $M$ on a smooth complex analytic variety $X$ of dimension $n$ is **holonomic** if its [[characteristic variety]] is of dimension $n$. It follows that the chracteristic variety of a holonomic D-module is conic and [[lagrangian submanifold|lagrangian]].

Holonomicity of D-modules is important also in [[geometric representation theory]].
