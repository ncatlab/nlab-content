## Idea

The classification of pure [[supergravity]] theories in dimensions 3,4,6,10 and 4,5,7,11 is parameterized by the real [[normed division algebras]], while the classification of [[super Yang-Mills theories]] is parameterized by pairs of real [[normed division algebras]], see at _[[supersymmetry and division algebra]]_ and at _[[spin representation]]_.

Combining this yields a classification of the extended super [[Einstein-Yang-Mills theories]] by triples of real normed division algebras. called a _magic pyramid_ of supergravity theories ([ABDHN 13](#ABDHN13)).

<img src="https://ncatlab.org/nlab/files/MagicPyramid.jpg" width = "600">

(from [ABDHN 13, p. 3](#ABDHN13))

In this diagram vertical axis labels the spacetime division algebra (hence vertical label $\mathbb{R}$ corresponds to $d = 2+1$, vertical label $\mathbb{C}$ to $d = 3+1$, $\mathbb{H}$ to $d = 5+1$ and $\mathbb{O}$ to $d = 9+1$) , while the horizontal axes label the algebras associated with the number of left and right supersymmetries. The entries display the corresponding [[U-duality]] groups. Each horizontal level is a "[[magic square]]".
