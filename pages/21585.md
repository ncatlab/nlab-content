## Idea

The [[homotopy 2-category]] $Ho_2\big((\infty,1)Cat\big)$ of the [[(∞,2)-category]] [[(∞,1)Cat]] of [[(∞,1)-categories]] has been argued ([Riehl & Verity 13](#RiehlVerity13)) to provide a useful context for [[(∞,1)-category theory]]. For example, the notion of [[adjoint (∞,1)-functors]] turns out to equivalently reduce to plain [[adjunctions]] in this homotopy 2-category (see [there](adjoint+(infinity,1)-functor#InTheHomotopy2Category) for more).

## Properties

### Relation to homotopy 2-category of model categories

One might expect (by the discussion [there](locally+presentable+(infinity%2C1)-category#PresentedByCombinatorialSimplicialModelCategories)) that the [[homotopy 2-category]] of [[locally presentable (∞,1)-categories]] is [[equivalence of 2-categories|equivalent]] to the [[localization of a 2-category|localization of the 2-category]] of [[combinatorial model categories]] at the [[Quillen equivalences]], [[Ho(CombModCat)]]. 

A full proof of this assertion, if indeed correct, seems not to have been written out.

However, on a closely related note, [Renaudin 09](#HoCombModCat#Renaudin06) proves an equivalence between [[Ho(CombModCat)]] and the [[homotopy 2-category]] of locally presentable [[derivators]].
