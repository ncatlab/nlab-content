Burchnall-Chaundy theory refers to ramifications of the theory of commuting differential operators. Under some assumptions commuting operators have to satisfy an algebraic relation (defining therefore an algebraic curve, __Burchnall-Chaundy curve__). The main origin is 1928 paper of Burchnall and Chaundy.
