##Â Idea

Given an [[endofunctor]] $F$ such that the [[category]] of [[algebra for an endofunctor|$F$-algebras]] has an [[initial object]] $(\mu F, in)$, the *catamorphism* for an $F$-algebra $(A, \phi)$ is the unique [[homomorphism]] from the [[initial algebra of an endofunctor | initial $F$-algebra]] $(\mu F, in)$ to $(A, \phi)$. The unique morphism between the carriers is also denoted $cata \phi : \mu F \rightarrow A$. From the commuting square of the homomorphism, we have $in \circ (cata \phi) = F (cata \phi) \circ \phi$.

\begin{centre}
\begin{tikzcd}
F (\mu F)
  \arrow[r, "F (\mathopen{\textrm{cata}} \phi)"]
  \arrow[d, "\textrm{in}"] &
F A
  \arrow[d, "\phi"] \\
\mu F
  \arrow[r, "(\mathopen{\textrm{cata}} \phi)"] &
A
\end{tikzcd}
\end{centre}
