## Idea

Given a [[metric space]] $(X,d)$ and a point $x \in X$, then the _unit sphere_ $S_x(X) \subset X$ is the [[subset]] of those points with unit [[distance]] from $x$:

$$
  S_x(X)
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \left\{
     x' \in X  \;\vert\; d(x',x) = 1
  \right\}
  \,.
$$ 

## Examples

In the [[Euclidean space]] $(X,d) = E^n$ of [[dimension]] $n$, the unit sphere is the usual [[n-sphere|(n-1)-sphere]] $S^{n-1} \simeq S_0(\mathbb{R}^n)$. For $n = 2$ this is the _unit [[circle]]_, for $n = 3$ the unit [[2-sphere]] and so on.
