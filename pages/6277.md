## Definition

In [[algebraic topology]] the Bernoulli numbers arise in the following fashion (e.g. [Adams 65, section 2](#Adams65)), notably as the order of some of the groups in the [[image of the J-homomorphism]].

There is the [[equation]] of [[functions]]/[[power series]] $x \mapsto f(x)$

$$
  \frac{x}{
    e^x -1
  }
  =
  \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \beta_k \frac{1}{k !}x^k
  \,.
$$

The $k$th Bernoulli number $B_k \in \mathbb{Q}$ is

$$
  B_k = (-1)^{k-1} \beta_{2k}
  \,.
$$
