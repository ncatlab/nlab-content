## Idea ##

In the same vein that [[commutative rings]] are to [[integral domains]], [[GCD rings]] are to [[GCD domains]], and [[prefields]] are to [[fields]], $\mathbb{N}$-[[N-graded prevector spaces|graded prevector spaces]] are to $\mathbb{N}$-[[graded vector spaces]]. 

## Definition ##

Given a [[prefield]] $F$, an **$\mathbb{N}$-graded $R$-prevector space** is an $R$-[[prevector space]] $V$ with a binary function $\langle - \rangle_{(-)}: A \times \mathbb{N} \to V$ called the **grade projection operator** such that

$$\prod_{v:V} v = \sum_{n:\mathbb{N}} \langle v \rangle_n$$

$$\prod_{a:F} \prod_{b:F} \prod_{v:V} \prod_{w:V} \prod_{n:\mathbb{N}} \langle a v + b w \rangle_n = a \langle v \rangle_n + b \langle w \rangle_n$$

$$\prod_{v:V} \prod_{n:\mathbb{N}} \langle \langle v \rangle_n \rangle_n = \langle v \rangle_n$$

$$\prod_{v:V} \prod_{m:\mathbb{N}} \prod_{n:\mathbb{N}} (m \neq n) \times (\langle \langle v \rangle_m \rangle_n = 0)$$

Terms of $V$ are called **multivectors**. 

For a natural number $n:\mathbb{N}$, the [[image]] of $\langle - \rangle_n$ under $V$ is called the **$n$-prevector space** and is denoted as $\langle V \rangle_n$. 

$$\langle V \rangle_n \coloneqq \mathrm{im}(\langle - \rangle_n)$$ 

The terms of $\langle V \rangle_n$ are called **$n$-vectors**. 

We define the **filtration operator** $\mathcal{F}_{(-)}: \mathbb{N} \times V \to V$

$$\mathcal{F}_{n}(v) = \sum_{m = 0}^n \langle v \rangle_m$$

For a natural number $n:\mathbb{N}$, the [[image]] of $\mathcal{F}_{n}$ under $V$ is called the **filtered $n$-prevector space** and is denoted as $\mathcal{F}_{n}(V)$. 

$$\mathcal{F}_{n}(V) \coloneqq \mathrm{im}(\mathcal{F}_{n})$$ 

The terms of $\mathcal{F}_{n}(V)$ are called **$n$-multiprevectors**. 

## Examples ##

* Every $\mathbb{N}$-graded $\mathbb{Q}$-vector space is a $\mathbb{N}$-graded $\mathbb{Q}$-prevector space. 

* Every $\mathbb{N}$-graded classical vector space is a $\mathbb{N}$-graded $F$-prevector space for a classical [[field]] $F$ (defined using [[denial inequality]]).

* Every $\mathbb{N}$-graded Heyting vector space is a $\mathbb{N}$-graded $F$-prevector space for a [[Heyting field]] $F$. 

* Every $\mathbb{N}$-graded discrete vector space is a $\mathbb{N}$-graded $F$-prevector space for a [[discrete field]] $F$. 

* Every $\mathbb{N}$-graded residue vector space is a $\mathbb{N}$-graded $F$-prevector space for a [[residue field]] $F$.
