Haskell Curry (b. September 12, 1900; d. September 1, 1982) was an American mathematician known for his work in developing [[combinatory logic]]. His name appears in concepts such as [[currying]] and the [[Curry-Howard correspondence]], as well as the computer programming language [[Haskell]].
