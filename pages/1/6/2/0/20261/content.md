## Definition

For $G$ a [[group]] and $H_1, H_2 \hookrightarrow$ two [[subgroups]], one says that they are _conjugate subgroups_ if there [[existential quantifier|exists]] an [[element]] $g \in G$ such that the [[conjugation action]] by $g$ takes $H_1 \to H_1$:

$$
  H_1 \sim_{conj} H_2
  \;\;\;
  \coloneqq
  \;\;\;
  H_2 = Ad_g(H_1) = g H_1 g^{-1}
  \,.
$$

The [[equivalence classes]] under this [[relation]] are hence called the _[[conjugacy classes]] of subgroups_ of $G$. These play a key role in much of [[group theory]] and [[representation theory]], for instance as parameters for [[group characters]].
