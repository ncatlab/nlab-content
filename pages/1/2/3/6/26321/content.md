## Definition

\begin{definition}
*In coordinates*, a *Hessian manifold* is a [[pseudo-Riemannian manifold]] $(M,g)$ that admits local coordinates $\{q^a\}$, called *special coordinates*, that realize the metric as the [[Hessian]] of a function $H$, the *Hessian potential*:
$$
g_{a,b} = \partial_{a} \partial_{b} H = H_{ab}
$$
such a pseudo-Riemannian metric $g$ is known as a *Hessian metric*.

The defining relation is only invariant under [[affine space|affine transformations]].

Equivalently, in a *coordinate-free expression* (see ([Shima (2013)](#Shima13))), a Hessian manifold is a pseudo-Riemannian manifold $(M,g)$ endowed with a torsion-free [[flat connection]] $\nabla$, such that the rank-3 tensor
$$
S=\nabla g
$$
is totally anti-symmetric.

\end{definition}
