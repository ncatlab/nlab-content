## Idea

The *2-sphere* $S^2$ is the ordinary [[sphere]] of [[dimension]] $2$, hence the [[n-sphere|$n$-sphere]] for $n = 2$. 

Equipped with its canonical [[complex structure]] this is known as the *[[Riemann sphere]]*.

## Properties

### Homotopy groups

\begin{proposition}\label{ThirdHomotopyGroup}
The 3rd [[homotopy group]] [[homotopy groups of spheres|of]] the [[2-sphere]] $S^2$ is freely generated by the [[complex Hopf fibration]] $h_{\mathbb{C}}$:

\[
  \pi_3\big(S^2\big) \simeq \mathbb{Z}
  \,.
\]
\end{proposition}
This is essentially due to [Hopf 1931 Satz I](#Hopf31). 
\begin{proof}
Once one knows that that 

1. $\pi_{n \geq 2}(S^1) = 0$

1. the [[complex Hopf fibration]] is a [[circle principal bundle]], [hence](Hurewicz+fibration#NumerableFiberBundlesAreSerreFibrations) a [[Serre fibration]] with [[fiber]] [[circle|$S^1$]], 

this follows by [[exact sequence|exactness]] of the corresponding [[long exact sequence of homotopy groups]]:

$$
  \underset{0}
  {\underbrace{
    \pi_3(S^1)
  }}
  \longrightarrow
  \underset{\mathbb{Z}}
  {\underbrace{
    \pi_3(S^3)
  }}
  \overset{ (h_{\mathbb{C}})_\ast }{\longrightarrow}
  \pi_3(S^2)
  \longrightarrow
  \underset{0}
  {\underbrace{
    \pi_2(S^1)
    \mathrlap{\,.}
  }}
$$

\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Under [[stabilization]] the complex Hopf fibration hence represents the [[first stable homotopy group of spheres]].
\end{remark}

In fact:

\begin{proposition}
*All* higher [[homotopy groups]] of the 2-sphere are non-trivial: $\pi_{\geq 2}(S^2) \neq 0$.
\end{proposition}
([Ivanov, Mikhailov & Wu 2016](#IvanovMikhailovWu2016)).
