## Definition

A [[Hopf algebra]] is called *involutive* (or *involutory*, e.g. [Kuperberg 91](#Kuperberg91), [Virelizier 02, Sec. 1.7](#Virelizier02)) if its [[antipode]] is an [[involution]].

Since the antipode of a Hopf algebra is necessarily an [[anti-homomorphism]] of the underlying [[associative algebra]]-structure (by [this Prop.](Hopf+algebra#AntipodeIsAnAntihomomorphism)), an involutive Hopf algebra is in particular a [[star algebra]].


## Examples

* [[group algebra]], [[groupoid algebra]], [[category algebra]] of a [[dagger-category]]

* [[homology of loop spaces]] under [[Pontrjagin product]]
