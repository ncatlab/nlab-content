## Definition

An **$\mathcal{A}$-group** or **affine group** or **antithesis group** is an [[A-set|$\mathcal{A}$-set]] $G$ with an [[A-function|$\mathcal{A}$-function]] $i:G \to G$, an $\mathcal{A}$-function $m:G \otimes G \to G$ from the [[tensor product of A-sets|tensor product]] $G \otimes G$ to $G$ and an element $e$ in $G$ such that $(G, \sim, e, m, i)$ is a [[groupal setoid]]. 

An $\mathcal{A}$-group $G$ is **strong** if $m$ is instead a function from the [[cartesian product of A-sets|cartesian product]] $G \times G$ to $G$. 

An $\mathcal{A}$-group $G$ is **commutative** or **abelian** if $(G, \sim, e, m)$ is a [[braided monoidal setoid]].
