## Idea 

The *Hartle-Hawking no-boundary-proposal* in [[quantum cosmology]] &lbrack;[Hawking 1982](#Hawking82), [Hartle & Hawking 1983](#HartleHawking83)&rbrack; is the informal assertion that [[wavefunctions]] $\Psi(X^3, g)$ on the space of spatial cosmological geometries $(X^3,g)$ are given by the [[path integral]] over all (partially [[Wick rotation|Wick-rotated]]) 4d geometries whose only [[manifold with boundary|boundary]] is $X^3$, hence which have *no boundary in the past*.

The idea is that this describes [[quantum states]] of the universe for those [[cosmology|cosmological]] evolutions where the universe appears "out of nothing".
