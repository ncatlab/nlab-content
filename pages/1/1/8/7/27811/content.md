## Idea

The notion of an *idempotent monoid object* is the generalization of the algebraic notion of *[[idempotent monoid]]* as one passes from the ambient [[category of sets]] [[internalization|into]] more general ambient [[categories]] with suitable properties.

## Definition

In a [[monoidal category with diagonals]] $(C, \otimes, I, \Delta)$, an **idempotent monoid object** is a [[monoid object]] $(M, \mu, \eta)$ such that $\mu \circ \Delta_M = \mathrm{id}_M$, where $\Delta_M$ is the [[diagonal morphism]] of $M$ and $\mathrm{id}_M$ is the [[identity morphism]] of $M$.
