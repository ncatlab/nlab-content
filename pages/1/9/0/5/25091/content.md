George Lakoff is a researcher into language, culture and cognition.

* wikipedia: [George Lakoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Lakoff)

Cognitive, [[linguistics|linguistic]] and cultural aspects of mathematics (which are also of relevance for learning) are emphasized in

* George Lakoff, Rafael E. N&#250;&#241;ez, _Where mathematics comes from_, Basic Books 2000, xviii+493 pp. [MR2001i:00013](http://www.ams.org/mathscinet-getitem?mr=1794854) (cf. also wikipedia: [George Lakoff#Mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Lakoff#Mathematics) and [Where Mathematics Comes From](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Where_Mathematics_Comes_From))

Cognitive role of metaphors is a recurring theme in works of Lakoff.

* George Lakoff, Mark Johnson, _Metaphors We Live By_, University of Chicago Press 1980; 2003. 2004 edition contains an 'Afterword' (see also wikipedia: [Metaphors We Live By](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metaphors_We_Live_By))

Lakoff is one of the founders of generative semantics (a viewpoint on syntactic analysis (see [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_semantics)) taking into account semantics as a basis).

* George Lakoff, _Toward a generative semantics_, 1963, 1976 [pdf](https://escholarship.org/content/qt64m2z2b1/qt64m2z2b1.pdf)
