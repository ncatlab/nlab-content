{:toc}

**Transcendental grammar** is the name given to applications of ideas inspired by Kant's trancendental philosophy to the theory of grammar. In a looser sense "transcendental linguistics" could be applied to any theory of language in the sphere around Kantian philosophy e.g. the language based criticism of Kant's account of pure reason by Hamann and Herder, the theory of language of [[Wilhelm von Humboldt]] or even the linguistic turn of Neokantianism in the writings of [[Ernst Cassirer]] and the early Walter Benjamin.
