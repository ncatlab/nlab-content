## Idea

Let $L$ be a [[field]], let $K \subseteq L$ be a subfield of $K$, and let $S \subseteq L$ be a [[subset]] of $L$. Then $S$ is **algebraically independent** from $K$ if for every element $\alpha \in S$, every polynomial function with coefficients in $K$ is equal to the zero polynomial function if it has $\alpha$ among its [[roots]] when interpreted in $L$ (in other words, $\alpha$ is [[transcendental element|transcendental]] over $K$).
