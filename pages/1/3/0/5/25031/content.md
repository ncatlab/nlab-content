## Idea

The [[image]] of the [[transgression]] operation from [[bundle gerbes]] ([[bundle gerbe with connection|with connection]]) to [[complex line bundles]] ([[connection on a vector bundle|with connection]]) on the [[free loop space]] of their base space may be characterized ([Waldorf 2009](#Waldorf09), [2010](#Waldorf10a), [2011](#Waldorf10b)) as consisting of bundles which are suitably compatible with the "fusion" of [[pairs]] of [[loops]] along [[thin homotopy|thin]] [[trinions]].

### Relation to string field theory?
 {#RelationToStringFieldTheory}

Curiously, the fusion operation that is formalized by the notion of fusion bundles is mathematically reminiscent of (and physically of essentially the same intuitive nature as)  the "star product" on closed string fields considered in [[string field theory]] &lbrack;[Witten (1986),Fig. 20](string+field+theory#Witten86)&rbrack;:
