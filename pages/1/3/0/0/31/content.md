[John Baez](http://math.ucr.edu/home/baez/) works on n-categories and physics.  He has written various papers on the interface between these subjects:

[Higher-dimensional algebra and topological quantum field theory](http://arxiv.org/abs/q-alg/9503002), with
James Dolan, Jour. Math. Phys. 36 (1995), 6073-6105.

[Higher-dimensional algebra I: braided monoidal 2-categories](http://math.ucr.edu/home/baez/bm2cat.ps), with Martin
Neuchl, Adv. Math. 121 (1996), 196-244.

[An introduction to $n$-categories](http://arxiv.org/abs/q-alg/9705009), in
<em>7th Conference on Category Theory and Computer Science</em>, eds.
E. Moggi and G. Rosolini, Springer Lecture Notes in
Computer Science vol. 1290, Springer, Berlin, 1997.

[Higher-dimensional algebra II: 2-Hilbert spaces](http://arxiv.org/abs/q-alg/9609018),
Adv. Math. 127 (1997), 125-189.

[Higher-dimensional algebra III: $n$-categories
and the algebra of opetopes](http://arxiv.org/abs/q-alg/9702014), with James Dolan, Adv. Math. 135 (1998), 145-206.

[Spin foam models](http://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9709052), Class. Quantum Grav. 15 (1998), 1827-1858.

[Categorification](http://arxiv.org/abs/math.QA/9802029), with James Dolan, in <em>Higher Category
Theory</em>, eds. Ezra Getzler and Mikhail Kapranov,
Contemp. Math. 230, American
Mathematical Society, Providence, Rhode Island, 1998, pp. 1-36.

[An introduction to spin foam models of
<em>BF</em> theory and quantum gravity](http://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9905087), in <em>Geometry and Quantum Physics</em>, eds. Helmut
Gausterer and Harald Grosse, Springer, Berlin, 2000,
pp. 25-93.

[From finite sets to Feynman diagrams](http://arxiv.org/abs/math.QA/0004133), with James Dolan, in <em>Mathematics Unlimited - 2001
and Beyond</em>, vol. 1, eds. Bj&ouml;rn Engquist and Wilfried Schmid, Springer,
Berlin, 2001, pp. 29-50.

[Higher-dimensional algebra and Planck-scale physics](http://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9902017),
in <em>Physics Meets Philosophy at the Planck Length</em>, eds.
Craig Callender and Nick Huggett, Cambridge U. Press, Cambridge, 2001,
pp. 177-195.  Also available as a [webpage](http://math.ucr.edu/home/baez/planck/).

[Higher-dimensional algebra IV: 2-tangles](http://arxiv.org/abs/math.QA/9811139), with Laurel Langford,
Adv. Math. 180 (2003), 705-764.

[Higher-dimensional
algebra V: 2-groups](http://arxiv.org/abs/math.QA/0307200), with Aaron D. Lauda, [Theory
and Applications of Categories](http://www.tac.mta.ca/tac/volumes/12/14/12-14abs.html) 12 (2004), 423-491.

[Higher-dimensional
algebra VI: Lie 2-algebras](http://arxiv.org/abs/math.QA/0307263), with Alissa S. Crans, [Theory
and Applications of Categories](http://www.tac.mta.ca/tac/volumes/12/15/12-15abs.html) 12 (2004), 492-528.

[Quantum quandaries:
a category-theoretic perspective](http://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0404040), in
<em>Structural Foundations of Quantum Gravity</em>,
eds. Steven French, Dean Rickles and Juha Saatsi, Oxford U. Press,Oxford, 2006, pp. 240-265. Also available as a [webpage](http://math.ucr.edu/home/baez/quantum/)

[Higher gauge theory](http://arxiv.org/abs/math.DG/0511710),
with Urs Schreiber, in
<em>Categories in Algebra, Geometry and Mathematical
Physics</em>, eds. Alexei Davydov, Michael Batanin,
Michael Johnson, Stephen Lack and Amnon Neeman, Contemp. Math. 431, American Mathematical Society, Providence, Rhode Island, 2007, pp. 7-30.

[From loop groups
to 2-groups](http://arxiv.org/abs/math.QA/0504123)
with Alissa S. Crans, Danny Stevenson and Urs Schreiber,
[Homotopy,
Homology and Applications](http://intlpress.com/HHA/v9/n2/a4/), 9 (2007), 101-135.

[Lectures on
n-categories and cohomology](http://arxiv.org/abs/math.CT/0608420), with Michael Shulman, to appear
in <em>n-Categories: Foundations and Applications</em>, eds.
John Baez and Peter May.

[The classifying space of a
topological 2-group](http://arxiv.org/abs/0801.3843), with Danny Stevenson, to appear in the
proceedings of the 2007 Abel Symposium, eds. Nils Baas <i>et al</i>.
