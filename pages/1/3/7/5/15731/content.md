## Idea

In [[quantum field theory]] (and [[string theory]]) the [[scattering amplitudes]] ([[string scattering amplitudes]]) "where nothing external scatters," hence for no incoming and no outgoing states, are called _[[vacuum]] amplitudes_. 

## Properties

### As generating functionals for all other amplitudes

As functions of [[source]] fields, the vacuum amplitudes, or rather the _[[vacuum energy]]_, serve as the [[generating functionals]] for all [[correlators]]/[[n-point functions]] (e.g. [Scrucca, 1.6](#Scrucca)).

### One loop contribution and zeta functions
 {#OneLoopContributionAndZetaFunction}

The 1-loop vacuum amplitudes are [[regularization (physics)|regularized]] [[traces]] over [[Feynman propagators]]/[[Dirac propagators]]. These are the incarnations of [[special values of L-functions|special values]] of [[zeta functions]], [[L-functions]] and [[eta functions]] in [[physics]]:

[[!include zeta-functions and eta-functions and theta-functions and L-functions -- table]]

### Vanishing of the vacuum amplitude and supersymmetry

In the presence of [[supersymmetry]] 1-loop vacuum amplitudes are typically supposed to vanish.

For the [[type II superstring]], see e.g. ([Palti](#Palti)). For the [[heterotic superstring]] see e.g. [Han 89](#Han89).

In view of the [above](#OneLoopContributionAndZetaFunction) relation of 1-loop vacuum amplitudes to [[special values of L-functions]] such vanishing reminds one of the [[Riemann hypothesis]]. See ([ACER 11](#ACER11)).
