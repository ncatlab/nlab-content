## Idea

*Persistent homotopy* studies [[homotopy types]] (of [[topological spaces]]) as a parameter (resolution) varies. Persistent homotopy theory is to [[homotopy theory]] as the more widely considered [[persistent homology]]  is to [[homology]].

See also the persistent *[[Cohomotopy|co-homotopy]]* [below](#CohomotopyInTopologicalDataAnalysisReferences).

In general, [[homotopy]] is a finer invariant than [[homology]]: the former sees the full [[homotopy type]] of a [[topological space]], the latter as most the underlying [[stable homotopy type]]. In other words, homology involves a kind of linearization or [[abelianization]] which loses information that is retained in the homotopy type. Therefore persistent homotopy is in general a finer invariant of [[filtered topological spaces]] (such as of interest in [[topological data analysis]]) than [[persistent homology]].
