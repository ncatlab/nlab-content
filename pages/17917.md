## Idea

In [[quantum electrodynamics]] there is a net [[force]] between two conducting but uncharged parallel plates, which is absent in the [[classical field theory]] of [[electromagnetism]]. This quantum effect is called the _Casimir effect_.

## References

Named after [[Hendrik Casimir]].

Discussion in the context of [[causal perturbation theory]] is in 

* {#HollandsWald14} [[Stefan Hollands]], [[Robert Wald]], pages 45-47 of _Quantum fields in curved spacetime_, Physics Reports Volume 574, 16 April 2015, Pages 1-35 ([arXiv:1401.2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/1401.2026))

* R. L. Jaffe, _The Casimir Effect and the Quantum Vacuum_, Phys.Rev. D72 (2005) 021301 ([arXiv:hep-th/0503158](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/0503158))
