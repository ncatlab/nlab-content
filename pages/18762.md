## Idea

The [[radius]] of the [[horizon]] of a [[Schwarzschild spacetime]] [[black hole]] of [[mass]] $m$ is called the _Schwarzschild radius_,

$$
  r_m  = 2 m G/c^2
$$

where $G$ denotes the [[gravitational constant]] and $c$ denotes the [[speed of light]].

## Properties


### Relation to Compton wavelength

Another [[physical unit]] of [[length]] parameterized by a mass $m$ is the _[[Compton wavelength]]_ $\ell_m = \frac{2 \pi \hbar}{m c}$. Solving the [[equation]]

  $$
    \array{
      & \ell_m &=& r_m
      \\
      \Leftrightarrow & 2\pi\hbar / m c &=& 2 m G / c^2
    }
  $$

  for $m$ yields the _[[Planck mass]]_

  $$
     m_{P} \coloneqq \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{\pi}} m_{\ell = r} = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c}{G}}
    \,.
  $$

  The corresponding [[Compton wavelength]] $\ell_{m_{P}}$ is given by the _[[Planck length]]_ $\ell_P$

  $$
    \ell_{P} \coloneqq \tfrac{1}{2\pi} \ell_{m_P} = \sqrt{ \frac{\hbar G}{c^3} }
    \,
  $$
