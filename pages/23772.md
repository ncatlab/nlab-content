## Definition 

For a [[commutative ring]] $R$, a **quadratic function** is a function  $f:R \to R$ with elements $a \in R$, $b \in R$, $c \in R$ such that for all $x \in R$,

$$f(x) = a \cdot x^2 + b \cdot x + c$$

where $x^2$ is the canonical [[square function]] of the multiplicative [[monoid]].
