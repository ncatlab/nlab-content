

### Classical model structure on simplicial sets

On the [[classical model structure on simplicial sets]]:

The original proof is due to

* {#Quillen67} [[Daniel Quillen]], Section II.3 of: _Axiomatic homotopy theory_ in: _[[Homotopical Algebra]]_, Lecture Notes in Mathematics 43, Springer 1967([doi:10.1007/BFb0097438](https://doi.org/10.1007/BFb0097438))

This proof is purely [[combinatorics|combinatorial]] (i.e. does not pass through [[geometric realization]] of [[simplicial sets]] as [[topological spaces]]): Quillen uses the theory of [[minimal Kan fibrations]], the fact that the latter are [[fiber bundles]], as well as the fact that the [[simplicial classifying space]] of a [[simplicial group]] is a [[Kan complex]]. 

Other proofs are were given in:

* {#GelfandManin96} [[Sergei Gelfand]], [[Yuri Manin]],  Sections V.1-2 of: _[[Methods of homological algebra]]_,  transl. from the 1988 Russian (Nauka Publ.) original, Springer 1996. xviii+372 pp. 2nd corrected ed. 2002 ([doi:10.1007/978-3-662-12492-5](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-662-12492-5))

* {#GoerssJardine99} [[Paul Goerss]], [[J. F. Jardine]], Section I.11 of: _[[Simplicial homotopy theory]]_, Progress in Mathematics, Birkh&#228;user (1999) Modern Birkh&#228;user Classics (2009) ([doi:10.1007/978-3-0346-0189-4](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-0346-0189-4), [webpage](http://web.archive.org/web/19990208220238/http://www.math.uwo.ca/~jardine/papers/simp-sets/))

* {#JoyalTierney08} [[André Joyal]], [[Myles Tierney]], _Notes on simplicial homotopy theory_, Lecture at _[Advanced Course on Simplicial Methods in Higher Categories](https://lists.lehigh.edu/pipermail/algtop-l/2007q4/000017.html)_, CRM 2008  ([[JoyalTierneyNotesOnSimplicialHomotopyTheory.pdf:file]])

* {#JoyalTierney09} [[André Joyal]], [[Myles Tierney]] _An introduction to simplicial homotopy theory_, 2005  ([web](http://hopf.math.purdue.edu/cgi-bin/generate?/Joyal-Tierney/JT-chap-01), [[JoyalTierneySimplicialHomotopyTheory.pdf:file]])



A proof (in fact two variants of it) using the [[Kan fibrant replacement]] $Ex^\infty$ functor is given (in the context of_[[Cisinski model structure]]_) in:

* {#Cisinski06} [[Denis-Charles Cisinski]], Section 2 of: _[[joyalscatlab:Les préfaisceaux comme type d'homotopie]]_, Ast&#233;risque, Volume 308, Soc. Math. France (2006), 392 pages ([pdf](http://www.math.univ-toulouse.fr/~dcisinsk/ast.pdf))
 
The crucial step is the proof that the fibrations are precisely the [[Kan fibrations]] (and also to prove all the good properties of $Ex^\infty$ without using topological spaces); for two different proofs of this fact using $Ex^\infty$, see Prop. 2.1.41 as well as Scholium 2.3.21 for an alternative). For the rest, everything was already in the book of Gabriel and Zisman, for instance.

Another approach using $Ex^\infty$ is:

* {#Moss} Sean Moss, _Another approach to the Kan-Quillen model structure_, Journal of Homotopy and Related Structures volume 15, pages 143–165 (2020) ([arXiv:1506.04887](http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.04887), [doi:10.1007/s40062-019-00247-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40062-019-00247-y))

A proof of the model structure not relying on the [[classical model structure on topological spaces]] nor on explicit models for [[Kan fibrant replacement]] is in

* [[Christian Sattler]], _The Equivalence Extension Property and Model Structures_ ([arXiv:1704.06911](https://arxiv.org/abs/1704.06911))

Proofs valid in [[constructive mathematics]] are given in:

* [[Simon Henry]], _A constructive account of the Kan-Quillen model structure and of Kan's Ex∞ functor_, [arXiv:1905.06160](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.06160).

* [[Nicola Gambino]], [[Simon Henry]], [[Christian Sattler]], [[Karol Szumiło]], _The effective model structure and ∞-groupoid objects_, [arXiv:2102.06146](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.06146).
 
As a [categorical semantics for homotopy type theory](relation+between+type+theory+and+category+theory#HomotopyTypeTheory), the model structure on simplicial sets is considered in

* {#KapulkinLumnsdaineVoevodsky12} [[Chris Kapulkin]], [[Peter LeFanu Lumsdaine]], [[Vladimir Voevodsky]], ([arXiv:1203.2553](http://arxiv.org/abs/1203.2553))

* [[Chris Kapulkin]], [[Peter LeFanu Lumsdaine]], *The Simplicial Model of Univalent Foundations (after Voevodsky)*, Journal of the European Mathematical Society  ([arXiv:1211.2851](https://arxiv.org/abs/1211.2851),[doi:10.4171/jems/1050](http://doi.org/10.4171/jems/1050))
