## Definition
The **reverse monoidal category** of a [[monoidal category]], $\mathcal{C}$, has the same underlying category and unit as $\mathcal{C}$ but reversed monoidal product, $X \otimes^{rev} Y = Y \otimes X$, and similarly for morphisms. The associator in the reverse category is $\alpha^{rev}_{X,Y,Z} = \alpha^{-1}_{Z,Y,X}$.

$V^{rev}$ is equivalent to $(\Sigma V)^{op}$ where $\Sigma V$ is the [[delooping]] of $V$, i.e. $V$ viewed as a one-object [[bicategory]] where ${op}$ is the [[opposite 2-category|opposite on 1-cells]].
