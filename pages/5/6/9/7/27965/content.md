## Definition


  For $\mathfrak{g}$ a [[Lie algebra]], its automorphism group $Aut(\mathfrak{g})$ is the group of [[general linear group|linear automorphisms]] of $\mathfrak{g}$ which preserve the Lie bracket. That is, for $A\in Aut(\mathfrak{g})$ and $x,y\in \mathfrak{g}$, it holds that

$$
A [x,y] = [A x, A y].
$$

The automorphism group $Aut(\mathfrak{g})$ of $\mathfrak{g}$ is not to be confused with its [[derivation Lie algebra]].

## Properties

There is a [[normal subgroup]] $Aut(\mathfrak{g})_0$ of automorphisms, , the _inner automorphisms_, obtained via the [[adjoint action]] as

$$
exp( ad(x)): \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g},
$$

for $x\in \mathfrak{g}$, giving rise to the [[exact sequence]]

$$
1 \to Aut(\mathfrak{g})_0 \to Aut(\mathfrak{g}) \to \pi_0 (Aut(\mathfrak{g})) \to 1.
$$

The [[quotient group]] $\pi_0 (Aut(\mathfrak{g}))$ of _outer automorphisms_ is discrete.

This sequence is known to be a [[group extension|split extension]] for $\mathfrak{g}$ a [[simple algebra|simple]] Lie algebra of finite dimension over the [[real number|reals]] or over the [[complex number|complex numbers]] ([Gündogan 10](#Gund10)).
