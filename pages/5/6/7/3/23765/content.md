## Definition

A function $f:\mathbb{R}_D \to \mathbb{R}_D$ is __locally nonconstant__ if for all [[Dedekind real numbers]] $x \lt y$ and $t \in \mathbb{R}_D$, there exists a Dedekind real number $z \in \mathbb{R}_D$ with $x \lt z \lt y$ and $\vert f(z) - t \vert \gt 0$. 

## Examples

Every [[monotonic function]] on the Dedekind real numbers is locally nonconstant.
