## Definition

Given indeterminates $t_1,\ldots, t_k$ a _Laurent polynomial_ over a [[field]] or [[ring]] $F$ is a [[polynomial]] in $t_1,\ldots,t_k,t_1^{-1},\ldots,t_k^{-1}$ over $F$. 

## Properties

### Ring of Laurent polynomials

Let $R$ be a [[commutative ring]], and let $S$ be a set. The ring of Laurent polynomials generated by $S$ is the [[localization of a commutative ring|localization]] of the [[polynomial ring]] $R[S]$, with canonical [[ring homomorphism]] $h:R \to R[S]$ and canonical function $i:S \to R[S]$, away from the image of $i$, $R[S, S^{-1}]$. 

Now, assume that $S$ is a [[singleton]] set $\{x\}$. The underlying $R$-[[module]] of the ring of Laurent polynomials $R[x, x^{-1}]$ is the [[integers object]] in the category $R$[[Mod]] of $R$-modules and $R$-[[linear maps]], with $c:R \to R[x, x^{-1}]$ taking [[scalars]] to constant Laurent polynomials and the [[linear isomorphism]] $P \mapsto P x:R[x, x^{-1}] \to R[x, x^{-1}]$ multiplying Laurent polynomials by $x$, with [[inverse function|inverse]] [[isomorphism]] $P \mapsto P x^{-1}:R[x, x^{-1}] \to R[x, x^{-1}]$.
