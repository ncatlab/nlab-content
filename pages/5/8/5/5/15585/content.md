This page is about:

* [[F. William Lawvere]]: 

  \linebreak

  **Functorial Semantics of Algebraic Theories** 

  \linebreak

  Ph.D. thesis

  Columbia University (1963)

  (advised by [[Samuel Eilenberg]]) 

which became highly influential in [[categorical algebra]], see *[[functorial semantics]]*.

The dissertation was not published at the time, and only two short notices appeared:

* [[F. William Lawvere]]: _Functorial Semantics of Algebraic Theories_, Proc. Nat.  Acad. Sci. **50** (1963) 869-872 &lbrack;[doi:10.1073/pnas.50.5.869](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.50.5.869), [pdf](http://www.pnas.org/content/50/5/869.full.pdf)&rbrack;

* [[F. William Lawvere]], *Algebraic theories, algebraic categories, and algebraic functors*, in: Addison, Henkin, Tarski (eds.), _The Theory of Models_, North-Holland Amsterdam (1965) 413-418 &lbrack;[doi:10.1016/B978-0-7204-2233-7.50044-7](https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-0-7204-2233-7.50044-7)&rbrack;

It was finally published together with an author's comment and a supplement here:

* {#Lawvere04} Reprints in Theory and Applications of Categories, No. 5 (2004) 1--121 &lbrack;[tac:tr4](http://www.tac.mta.ca/tac/reprints/articles/5/tr5abs.html)&rbrack;

In his thesis, Lawvere introduces what came to be called *[[Lawvere theories]]* and the [[categorical semantics|functorial perspective]] into [[model theory]]. He also takes steps towards axiomatizing [[ETCC|the category of categories as a foundation for mathematics]]. This  includes introducing the notion of [[comma category]] as an auxiliary to a definition of [[adjoint functors|adjunction]] implicitly involving an isomorphism of [[2-sided discrete fibrations]] (cf. [Lawvere 2004](#Lawvere04) pp.12--13).

Generalization to relational theories:

* [[Filippo Bonchi]], [[Dusko Pavlovic]], [[Pawel Sobocinski]], _Functorial Semantics for Relational Theories_, &lbrack;[arXiv:1711.08699](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.08699)&rbrack;

* [[Chad Nester]]: _A Variety Theorem for Relational Universal Algebra_, Lecture Notes in Computer Science **13027** (2021) 362-377 &lbrack;[arXiv:2105.04958](https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.04958), [doi:10.1007/978-3-030-88701-8_22](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-88701-8_22)&rbrack;

* [[Chad Nester]]: *Partial And Relational Algebraic Theories*, PhD thesis, Tallin University (2024) &lbrack;[doi:10.23658/taltech.4/2024](https://doi.org/10.23658/taltech.4/2024)&rbrack;

Generalization to partial algebraic theories:

* [[Ivan Di Liberti]], [[Fosco Loregian]], [[Pawel Sobocinski]], [[Chad Nester]]: *Functorial semantics for partial theories* &lbrack;[arXiv:2011.06644](https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.06644)&rbrack;
