## Idea

The *annular braid group* or *circlular braid group* $CBr_n$ (cf. [Bellingeri & Bodin 2014 p 1](#BellingeriBodin14)) on $n \in \mathbb{N}$ strands is the [[surface braid group]] of the [[annulus]], hence the [[fundamental group]] of the [[configuration space of points|configuration space of $n$-points]] inside the [[annulus]].

## Properties

\begin{definition}
  \label{AffineBraidGroup}
  The *affine type A* braid group $Br_n^{aff}$ has generators and relations given by the same formulas as for the [Artin presentation](braid+group#ArtinPresentation) of the ordinary [[braid group]] $Br_n$, except that the indices in these formulas are identified [[modulo]] $n$.
\end{definition}


\begin{proposition}
  The annular braid group is [[isomorphism|isomorphic]] to a [[semidirect product group|semidirect product]] 
\[
  \label{AnnularBraidGroupAsSemidirectProductOfAffineWithInt}
  CBr_n
  \,\simeq\,
  Br_n^{aff} 
    \rtimes 
  \underset
  {
    \mathbb{Z}
  }
  {
    \underbrace{
      \langle c \rangle
    }
  }
\]
of the affine braid group (Def. \ref{AffineBraidGroup}) generated by Artin Generators $b_i$ with the group of [[integers]], the latter generated by the braid $c$ which exhibits a 1-step [[cyclic permutation]] around the inner boundary of the annulus.
\end{proposition} 
([Kent & Pfeifer 2002 Thm 1](#KentPfeifer02), cf. [Gadbled, Thiel & Wagner 2015 Rem. 1.1](#GadbledThielWagner15)).

<img src="https://ncatlab.org/nlab/files/CyclicBraidingOnPuncturedAnnulus.jpg" width="700">


\begin{remark}
  The [[mapping class group]] of the $n$-punctured [[annulus]] the further [[semidirect product]] of (eq:AnnularBraidGroupAsSemidirectProductOfAffineWithInt) with another copy of $\mathbb{Z}$:
$$
  MCG(\mathbb{A}, n)
  \,\simeq\,
  CBr_n \rtimes \mathbb{Z}
  \,\simeq\,
  Br_n^{aff} \rtimes \mathbb{Z}^2
  \,.
$$
\end{remark}
([Gadbled, Thiel & Wagner 2015 Rem. 1.2](#GadbledThielWagner15))

In fact, it should even be a *direct* product
$$
  MCG(\mathbb{A}, n)
  \,\simeq\,
  CBr_n \times 
  \underset
  { \mathbb{Z} }
  {
    \underbrace{
      \langle d\rangle
    }
  }
$$
because (by arguments as in [Bellingeri & Gervais 2012](#braid+group#BellingeriGervais12), cf. [MO:q/488784](https://mathoverflow.net/q/488784/381)) the extra generator $d$ (the annulus *Dehn twist*) may be taken to leave all the punctures fixed:
