Moment map came up historically as a generalization of an angular momentum in the dynamics of rigid bodies. 

the basic setup is the following: Let $M$ be a [[symplectic manifold]] with a [[Hamiltonian action]] of a Lie group $G$. That means that there is an action $\nu : G\times M\to M$ via symplectomorphisms, and...


Moment map is a crucial ingredient in the construction of Marsden-Weinstein [[symplectic quotient]]s.
