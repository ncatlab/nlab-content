Mathematics Subject Classification is a classification system for subjects of mathematics.

Each code consists of three parts; the first two digits denote the broadest discipline of the subject. The following one digit denotes the narrower topic, while the last two digits denote the most specific subject.

## Examples

A few examples are listed below based on MSC2020. Each discipline and topic, if appropriate, is linked to a corresponding article.

- 18-XX: [[category theory]], [[homological algebra]]
  - 18Axx: general theory of categories and functors
    - 18A25: [[functor category]], [[comma category]]
    - 18A40: [[adjoint functor]]
  - 18Bxx: special categories
    - 18B25: [[topos|topoi]]
    - 18B40: [[groupoid]], [[semigroupoid]], [[semigroup]], [[group]]
  - 18Dxx: categorical structures
    - 18D20: [[enriched category]]
  - 18E05: [[preadditive category]], [[additive category]]
  - 18E08: [[regular category]], [[Barr-exact category]]
  - 18E10: [[abelian category]], [[Grothendieck category]]
  - 18Fxx: categories in geometry and topology
    - 18F75: [[quantale]]
  - 18Mxx: monoidal categories and operads
    - 18M05: [[monoidal category]], [[symmetric monoidal category]]
  - 18Nxx: [[higher category|higher categories]] and [[homotopical algebra]]
    - 18N10: [[2-category]], [[bicategory]], [[double category]]
    - 18N25: [[horizontal categorification|categorification]]
    - 18N50: [[simplicial set]], [[simplicial object]]
    - 18N65: [[(infinity,n)-category|(∞, n)-category]], [[infinity-category|(∞, ∞)-category]]
