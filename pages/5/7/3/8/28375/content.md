
Primetry**
 is a concept introduced by Ryosuke Handa (2026) to denote
the singular, irreducible fold of space at which
symmetry and  asymmetry make contact
and asymmetry is generated.

A primetry is identified with a prime quantity fold:
the n-th irreducible passage-point of asymmetry generation
in a continuous spatial substrate.

Unlike symmetry (invariance under transformation)
and asymmetry (violation of such invariance),
primetry designates the *point of generation itself*:
the site where the transition from symmetry to asymmetry
is irreducibly accomplished.

The mathematical signature of primetry is the Gate:
the unique point on the unit circle at which the prenumber rotation
arrives at the real axis on its negative side,
generating the prime quantity excess n > 0

Etymology
The word *primetry* is formed from *prime*
(as in prime number/ prime quantity: first, irreducible)
and the suffix *-metry* (from Greek *metron*, measure,
as in geometry, symmetry, asymmetry).
