## Idea

To every nonsingular [[algebraic curve]] $C$ (over the [[complex numbers]]) of [[genus of a surface|genus]] $g$ one associates the _Jacobian variety_ or simply _Jacobian_ $J(C)$, either via [[differential 1-forms]] or equivalently via [[line bundles]]: the Jacobian is the [[moduli space]] of [[degree of a coherent sheaf|degree]]-$0$ [[line bundles]] over $C$, i.e. the [[connected component]] 

$$
  Jac(X) = Pic_0(X)
$$ 

of the [[neutral element]] of the [[Picard scheme]] of $C$. See also at _[intermediate Jacobian -- Examples -- Jacobian](intermediate+Jacobian#ExamplePicard)_.

Jacobian varieties are the most important class of [[abelian varieties]]. 


## Properties

### Abel-Jacobi map

The __[[Abel-Jacobi map]]__ $C\to J(C)$ is defined with help of [[periods]].

### Line bundles and theta functions

Over the [[complex numbers]], [[line bundles]] on a Jacobian variety over a given [[Riemann surface]] are naturally encoded by [[Riemann theta functions]].
