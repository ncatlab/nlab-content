## Idea

Under the [[function field analogy]] it makes sense to regard a [[number field]] as the [[rational function|rational functions]] on an "[[arithmetic curve]] over [[F1]]". Accordingly, there is a sensible version of the concept of [[genus of a curve]] for number fields. 

This _genus of a number field_ was originally introduced in ([Weil 39](#Weil39)) in a maybe somewhat ad hoc way. It is derived as being that definition which makes the [[Riemann-Roch theorem]] come out right in ([Neukirch 92, chapter III, def. (3.5)](#Neukirch92)).

## Examples

* The genus of the [[rational numbers]] themselves is 0.
