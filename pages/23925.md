## Idea ##

The [[moment]] of a [[force]]. 

##Â Definition ##

### For point particles ###

Given a point particle $P$ in an Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^n$, an associated lever arm vector $\overrightarrow{r}:\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}^n$ and a force $\overrightarrow{f}:\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}^n$ acting on $P$, the **torque** or **moment of a force** $M$ is the wedge product of the lever arm vector and the force:

$$M(t) = \overrightarrow{r}(t) \wedge \overrightarrow{f}(t)$$ 

Thus, torque is a [[bivector]].
