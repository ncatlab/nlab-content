## Definition

If $W$ is a [[Coxeter group]] of finite rank then a __Coxeter element__, sometimes also called Coxeter transformation, is a product of all simple reflections in any order. Notice that this is not the same as the longest Coxeter element (the maximal element in the Bruhat order). 

One talks about __Coxeter transformations__ also in a number of more general situations. We say that a finite rank abelian group $A$ is a bilinear group if it is equipped with a (typically nonsymmetric) biadditive map
$$
\langle -,-\rangle : A\times A\to\mathbf{Z}
$$
and an automorphism $\tau:A\to A$, called the Coxeter transformation, satisfying 
$$
\langle y, x\rangle = -\langle x, \tau y\rangle,\,\,\,x,y\in A.
$$
If $\langle -,-\rangle$ is nondegenerate then it follows that $A$ is torsion free hence a lattice, and we say that it is a __bilinear lattice__.

## Properties

If $A$ is a bilinear lattice then $ \tau$ belongs to the center of $Aut(A)$.
