## Idea

_Quantum chromodynamics_ ("QCD") is the [[quantum field theory]] of [[Yang-Mills theory]]: it describes the [[quantum theory]] of  [[gluons]] and [[quarks]].

The corresponding [[effective field theory]] that describes [[bound states]] such as [[protons]] is [[quantum hadrodynamics]].

### Lattice QCD checks

Due to _[[confinement]]_, the fundamental degrees of freedom in terms of which QCD is formulated, namely the [[quarks]], are actually not the low-energy [[bound states]] of the theory, which instead are the [[hadrons]]. This leaves room to speculate that QCD is not really the fundamental theory of the [[strong nuclear force]].

However, brute-force computation in [[lattice QCD]] shows that the [[quark]]-model does reproduce these [[hadron]] [[bound states]] _somehow_ (even if the real understanding of how it does so remains open, this is the _[[mass gap problem]]_):

* S. Durr, Z. Fodor, J. Frison, C. Hoelbling, R. Hoffmann, S.D. Katz, S. Krieg, T. Kurth, L. Lellouch, T. Lippert, K.K. Szabo, G. Vulvert,

  _Ab-initio Determination of Light Hadron Masses_,

  Science 322:1224-1227,2008 ([arXiv:0906.3599](https://arxiv.org/abs/0906.3599))

  conclusion on p. 4:
  
> our study strongly suggests that [[QCD]] is the theory of the [[strong nuclear force|strong interaction]], at low energies as well

* {#FodorHoelbling12} Zoltan Fodor, Christian Hoelbling, _Light Hadron Masses from Lattice QCD_, Rev. Mod. Phys. 84, 449, ([arXiv:1203.4789](https://arxiv.org/abs/1203.4789))

* S. Aoki et. al. _Review of lattice results concerning low-energy particle physics_ ([arXiv:1607.00299](https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.00299))
