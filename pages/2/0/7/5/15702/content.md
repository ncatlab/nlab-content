## Idea

An _Artin L-function_ is an [[L-function]] induced from an $n$-dimensioan [[linear representation]] 

$$
  \sigma\;\colon\; Gal \longrightarrow GL_n(\mathbb{C})
$$

of the [[Galois group]]: it is the product ("[[Euler product]]") over all [[prime ideals]] $p$ of, essentially, the [[characteristic polynomials]] of the [[Frobenius homomorphism]] $Frob_p$ ([e.g. Gelbhart 84, II.C.2](#Gelbhart84)).

For $n = 1$ then for each 1-dimensional [[Galois representation]] $\sigma$ there is a _[[Dirichlet character]]_ $\chi$ such that the Artin L-function $L_\sigma$ is equal to the [[Dirichlet L-function]] $L_\chi$ -- this relation is part of _[[Artin reciprocity]]_.

More generally, for $n \geq 1$ the [[conjecture]] of _[[Langlands correspondence]]_ is that for each $n$-dimensional [[Galois representation]] $\sigma$ there is an [[automorphic representation]] $\pi$ such that the Artin L-function $L_\sigma$ equals the [[automorphic L-function]] $L_\pi$.

(e.g [Gelbhart 84, pages 5-6](#Gelbhart84))
