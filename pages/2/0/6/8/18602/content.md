## Idea

In [[perturbative quantum field theory]], the [[magnetic moment]] of [[particles]] as predicted by [[classical field theory]] may receive corrections due to [[quantum physics|quantum effects]]. Such corrections are also called _[[quantum anomalies]]_, and hence one speaks of the _anomalous magnetic moments_.

The archetypical example is the anomalous magnetic moment of the [[electron]] in [[quantum electrodynamics]], which is famous as the [[pQFT]]-prediction that matches [[experiment]] to an accuracy of about $10^{-12}$ (e.g.[Scharf 95, (3.10.20)](#Scharf95)).
