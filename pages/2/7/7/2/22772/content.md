## Definition

In [[probability theory]] an [[event (probability theory)|event]] of [[probability]] 1 is said to happen *almost surely*.

That is,
> A statement $S$ about outcomes is said to be true *almost surely*, or *with probability 1*, if

$F := \{ \omega : S(\omega) \ \text{is true} \ \} \in \mathcal{F}$ and $\mathbf{P}(F) = 1$.

Where $\omega$ is a point in the sample space $\Omega$, $\mathcal{F}$ a [[sigma algebra|$\sigma$-algebra]] on $\Omega$, and $\mathbf{P}$ a [[probability measure]] on $(\Omega$, $\mathcal{F})$. (Williams 1991)

In [[measure theory]], such subsets are also known as [[full subsets]].
Their [[complements]] are known as [[null subsets]] or [[negligible subsets]].
