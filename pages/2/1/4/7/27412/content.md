## Definition

An **$\mathcal{A}$-ring** or **affine ring** or **antithesis ring** is an [[A-set|$\mathcal{A}$-set]] $R$ with an [[A-function|$\mathcal{A}$-function]] $-:R \to R$, an $\mathcal{A}$-function $(-)+(-):R \otimes R \to R$ from the [[tensor product of A-sets|tensor product]] $R \otimes R$ to $R$, an element $0$ in $R$, an [[A-function|$\mathcal{A}$-function]] $(-)\cdot(-):R \otimes R \to R$ from the [[tensor product of A-sets|tensor product]] $R \otimes R$ to $R$ and an element $1$ in $R$ such that $(R, \sim, 0, +, -, 1, \cdot)$ is a [[ring setoid]]. 

An $\mathcal{A}$-ring $R$ is 

* **additively strong** if $(-)+(-)$ is instead a function from the [[cartesian product of A-sets|cartesian product]] $R \times R$ to $R$. 

* **multiplicatively strong** if $(-)\cdot(-)$ is instead a function from the [[cartesian product of A-sets|cartesian product]] $R \times R$ to $R$. 

* **strong** if $R$ is both additively strong and multiplicatively strong. 

An $\mathcal{A}$-ring $R$ is **commutative** if $(R, \sim, 1, \cdot)$ is a [[braided monoidal setoid]].
