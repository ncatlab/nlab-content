## Idea
**Construction in philosophy** is the name of an article by F. W. J. Schelling in   '_Kritisches Journal der Philosophie_' (1802) that was the preliminary culmination point of a development in philosophy going back to Descartes and Spinoza and forcefully updated by I. Kant in '_Metaphysische Anfangsgr&#252;nde der Naturwissenschaft_' (1786) , namely the attempt to philosophize '_more geometrico_'.

In the 20th century this tradition has been revitalized partly by the school of phenomenology and in the context of analytical philosophy by M. Dummett.
