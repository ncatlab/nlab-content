## Idea

A [[continuous function]] between [[topological spaces]] which respects a given [[cell complex]]-[[mathematical structure|structure]] on these spaces is called a _cellular map_.

## Definition

Let $X$ and $Y$ be [[CW-complexes]] and let $X_n$ (respectively $Y_n$ ) denote the $n$-[[skeleton]] of $X$ (respectively $Y$). Then a [[continuous function]] $f:X \rightarrow Y$ is said to be _cellular_ if it takes $n$-skeletons to $n$-skeletons for all $n = 0,1,2,...,$ i.e, if

$$
  f(X_n ) \subseteq Y_n
$$

for all [[natural numbers]] $n$.
