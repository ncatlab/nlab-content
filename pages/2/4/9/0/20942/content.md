Let $X$ and $Y$ be CW-complexes and let $X_n$ (respectively $Y_n$ ) denote the $n$-skeleton of $X$ (respectively $Y$). Then a continuous map $f:X \rightarrow Y$ is said to be cellular if it takes $n$-skeletons to $n$-skeletons for all $n = 0,1,2,...,$ i.e, if

 $f(X_n ) \subseteq Y_n$, 
for all nonnegative integers $n$.
