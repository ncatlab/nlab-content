__Gel'fand-Raikov theorem__ (&#1043;&#1077;&#1083;&#1100;&#1092;&#1072;&#1085;&#1076;-&#1056;&#1072;&#1081;&#1082;&#1086;&#1074;) The [[irreducible representation|irreducible]] [[unitary representation]]s of a [[locally compact topological group]] $G$ separates its points. In other words, for any two group elements $g,h\in G$ there exist an irreducible unitary representation $\rho : G\to U(H)$ such that $\rho(g)\neq \rho(h)$.
