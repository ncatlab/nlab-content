## Idea
 {#Idea}

It seems ([Nair 1988](#Nair88), [Witten 2004, Sec. 3](#Witten04)) that the [[scattering amplitudes]] of $n$ [[gluons]] ([[n-point functions]]) in pure [[Yang-Mills theory]] on 4d [[Minkowski spacetime]], regarded as [[distributions]] on the [[product space]] of $n$ copies of [[twistor space]] [[complex projective 3-space|$\mathbb{C}P^3$]], have [[support of a distribution|support]] -- after [[complexification]] -- on (the [[diagonal morphism|diagonal]] [[image]] of) a [[complex curve]] in [[twistor space]], hence on the [[image]] of a [[holomorphic function]] 

\[
  \label{ComplexCurveInTwistorSpace}
  \phi \;\colon\; \Sigma \longrightarrow \mathbb{C}P^3
\] 

from a [[Riemann surface]] $\Sigma$ to [[complex projective 3-space]]. Here the [[genus of a surface|genus]] $g$ of $\Sigma$ is bounded by the [[loop order]] $l$ of the [[scattering amplitude]],  $g \leq l$ and the [degree](homotopy+of+rational+maps#eq:DegreeOfAMapToCPn) of the map (eq:ComplexCurveInTwistorSpace) depends on the number $q$ of [[negative number|negative]] [[helicity]] gluon states in the process, as $d \,=\, q - 1 + l$.

The idea of [[twistor string theory]] is to understand these [[complex curves]] in [[twistor space]] as [[worldsheet]] [[instantons]] (or rather [[D1-brane]]-instantons) of a [[string theory]] -- specifically of the [[B-model]] [[topological string]], whose [[target space]] is the [[twistor space]] [[complex projective 3-space|$\mathbb{C}P^3$]], or rather the [[supermanifold]] $\mathbb{C}^{3\vert 4}$ -- and to use this to explain the structure of [[MHV amplitudes]] in [[N=4 D=4 super Yang-Mills theory]].

Somehow. The original proposals ([Witten 04](#Witten04), [Berkovits 04](#Berkovits04)) were only partially succesful as [[string theories]] (e.g. [Skinner 09](#Skinner09), [Spradlin](#Spradlin)). 

But the idea as such of [[scattering amplitudes]] localized on [[complex curves]] in [[twistor space]] remains fruitful in pure [[quantum field theory]], for instance it seems to generalize to [[perturbative quantum gravity]] in the form of [[D=4 N=8 supergravity]] ([Cachazo & Skinner 2012](#CachazoSkinner12)).
