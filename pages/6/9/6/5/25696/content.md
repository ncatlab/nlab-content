## Idea

A generalization of the notion of *[[concrete category]]* from [[category theory]] to [[(infinity,1)-category theory]]. 

## Definition 

\begin{definition}
A __concrete (infinity,1)-category__ is a [[(infinity,1)-category]] $C$ equipped with an [[k-surjective functor|essentially $\infty$-surjective]] [[(infinity,1)-functor]]
  
  $$
    U \colon C \to \infty Grpd
  $$

to the [[(infinity,1)-category]] $\infty Grpd$ of [[infinity-groupoids]]. However every (infinity,1)-functor functor is essentially $\infty$-surjective, so the surjectivity requirement is redundant. We say an (infinity,1)-category $C$ is _concretizable_ if and only if it admits a [[(infinity,1)-functor]] $U \colon C \to \infty Grpd$. 
\end{definition}
