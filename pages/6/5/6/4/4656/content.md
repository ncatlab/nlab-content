A __composable pair of morphisms__ in a given [[category]] $C$ consists of [[objects]] $X,Y,Z$ of $C$ and [[morphisms]] $f\colon X \to Y$ and $g\colon Y \to Z$.  The [[composite]] of this composable pair is the morphism $g \circ f\colon X \to Z$.

A composable pair in $C$ is precisely a $2$-simplex in the [[nerve]] of $C$.

Sometimes one defines a composable pair to be a literal [[ordered pair|pair]] $(f,g)$ such that the [[target]] of $f$ is equal to the [[source]] of $g$, but this is (prima facie) [[evil]].
