## Idea

_Differential Galois theory_ is an analogue of [[Galois theory]] where [[fields]] are generalized to [[differential fields]].

## References

Expositions are in

* Andy Magid, _Differential Galois theory_, Notices of the AMS (1999) ([pdf](http://www.ams.org/notices/199909/fea-magid.pdf))

* Teresa Crespo, _The origins of differential Galois theory_ ([pdf slides](http://atlas.mat.ub.es/personals/crespo/Origins.pdf))

Textbook accounts include

* Marius van der Put, Michael Singer, _Galois Theory of Linear Differential Equations_, Springer Berlin (2003)

* M. Singer, _Direct and inverse problems in differential Galois theory_, in H. Bass, et al. (eds.) _Selected Works of Ellis Kolchin with Commentary_ , American Mathematical Society, Providence, RI, 1999, pp. 527&#8211;554.

Discussion in terms of [[D-geometry]] is in 

* [[João Pedro P. dos Santos]], _The behaviour of the differential Galois group on the generic and special fibres: A Tannakian approach_,  J. reine angew. Math. 637 (2009), 63-98 ([pdf](http://www.math.jussieu.fr/~dos-santos/BDGG_gen_special_V2.pdf))

* [[João Pedro P. dos Santos]], _Lifting D-modules from positive to zero characteristic_ ([pdf](http://www.math.jussieu.fr/~dos-santos/V2_22_2_2010.pdf))

based on

* B. H. Matzat, M. van der Put, _Iterative differential equations and the
Abhyankar conjecture_, J. Reine Angew. Math. 557 (2003), pp. 1&#8211;52.
