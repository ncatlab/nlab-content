Let $n\gt 2$. The Drinfel'd-Kohno Lie algebra is a $\mathbf{Z}$-algebra $L_n$ defined by generators $t_{ij} = t_{ji}$, $1\leq i\neq j\leq n$ subject to the relations 

$$
[t_{ij}, t_{kl}] = 0, \,\,\,[t_{ij}, t_{ik}+t_{jk}] = 0, \,\,\,\,\,1\leq i\neq j\neq k\neq l\leq n
$$

The corresponding algebra over rationals $L_n\otimes \mathbf{Q}$ is isomorphic to the Lie algebra of the prounipotent completion of the pure braid group $PB_{n-1}$; the universal enveloping $U(L_n)\otimes \mathbf{Q}$ is a Koszul algebra. 

Cf. [[Drinfeld-Kohno theorem]]
