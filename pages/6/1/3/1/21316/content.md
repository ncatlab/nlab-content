# Eilenberg-Zilber categories

* table of contents
{: toc}

## Idea

*Eilenberg-Zilber categories* are a special sort of [[generalized Reedy categories]] for which degeneracy maps behave particularly well.

## Definition

\begin{definition}\label{EZCategory}
An **Eilenberg-Zilber category** (or **EZ-category**) is a [[small category]] $R$ equipped with a [[function]] $d \colon ob(R) \to \mathbb{N}$ such that

1. For $f \colon x\to y$ a [[morphism]] of $R$:

   1. If $f$ is an [[isomorphism]], then $deg(x)=deg(y)$.

   2. If $f$ is a [[invertible morphism|noninvertible]] [[monomorphism]], then $deg(x)\lt deg(y)$.

   3. If $f$ is a noninvertible [[split epimorphism]], then $deg(x) \gt deg(y)$.

2. Every morphism factors as a [[split epimorphism]] followed by a [[monomorphism]].

3. Any [[pair]] of split epimorphisms in $R$ has an [[absolute colimit|absolute]] [[pushout]].

\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
Since a morphism is a [[split epimorphism]] if and only if its [[image]] in the [[presheaf category]] $[R^{op},Set]$ is an [[epimorphism]], condition (2) in Def. \ref{EZCategory} says that the [[(epi, mono) factorization system]] of $[R^{op},Set]$ restricts to $R$ via the [[Yoneda embedding]], while condition (3) says that the [[representable functor|representables]] are closed in $[R^{op},Set]$ under [[pushouts]] of pairs of epimorphisms.
\end{remark}

## Properties

Any EZ-category is a [[generalized Reedy category]] where $R^+$ and $R^-$ are the monomorphisms and the split epimorphisms, respectively.  Moreover, $R^{op}$ is also a generalized Reedy category where the definitions of $R^+$ and $R^-$ are reversed.  However, the generalized Reedy model structures on contravariant functors (corresponding to the generalized Reedy structure on $R^{op}$) are generally better-behaved.

Any element of a presheaf on an EZ-category $R$ is a degeneracy of a unique nondegenerate element.

If an EZ-category is also a strict [[Reedy category]] (i.e. contains no nonidentity isomorphisms), then it is an [[elegant Reedy category]].

## Examples

\begin{example}
The [[category of simplices]] $\Delta$ is an Eilenberg–Zilber category.
\end{example}

\begin{example}
The [[wreath product]] of $\Delta$ and an EZ-category (also known as the $\Theta$-construction) is again an EZ-category (Bergner–Rezk, Proposition 4.3).
In particular, Joyal's category $\Theta_n$ is an EZ-category for all $n\ge0$.
\end{example}

\begin{example}
[[Segal's category]] $\Gamma$ (used to define [[Gamma-spaces]]) is an EZ-category ([Berger & Moerdijk 2011](#BergerMoerdijk2011), Examples 6.8).
\end{example}

\begin{example}
The category of [[symmetric simplices]] (inhabited finite sets and their maps) is an EZ-category ([Berger & Moerdijk](#BergerMoerdijk2011), Examples 6.8).
\end{example}

\begin{example}
The [[cyclic category]] $\Lambda$ and the [[category of trees]] $\Omega$ are EZ-categories ([Berger & Moerdijk](#BergerMoerdijk2011), Examples 6.8).
\end{example}

\begin{example}
More generally, the total category $\mathbf{R}G$ of a
crossed group $G$ on an EZ-category $\mathbf{R}$ whose underlying Reedy category is strict
is itself an EZ-category ([Berger & Moerdijk](#BergerMoerdijk2011), Examples 6.8).
\end{example}

\begin{example}
The [[category of cubes]] $Q$ (generated by faces and degeneracies, without connections, symmetries, reversals, or diagonals) is an EZ-category ([Isaacson 2010](#Isaacson2010), Proposition 4.4).
\end{example}

\begin{example}
The category of symmetric cubes with min-connections ([Isaacson](#Isaacson2010), Definition 3.4, Proposition 3.11) is an EZ-category ([Isaacson](#Isaacson2010), Proposition 4.4).
\end{example}

\begin{example}
The [[cartesian cube category]] is an EZ-category ([Campion](#Campion2023), Theorem 8.12(1)).
\end{example}
