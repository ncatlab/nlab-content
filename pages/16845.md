## Idea 

The category of [[pointed object|pointed objects]] $1\backslash \mathcal{E}$ of a topos $\mathcal{E}$ has [[zero object|zero objects]] hence can be the degenerate topos at best. By altering the notion of morphism it is nevertheless possible to obtain a topos $^\bullet\mathcal{E}$ with objects $1\to X$, called the **topos of pointed objects**.

## Definition

Let $\mathcal{E}$ be a topos. The _topos $^\bullet\mathcal{E}$ of pointed objects_ has objects the morphisms $1\to X$ and morphisms pulback squares:

$$
\begin{array}{cccc}1& \to  & X   \\
\downarrow     &                   & \downarrow \\
1       & \to & Y
\end{array}

$$

## Properties

* Foremost, $^\bullet\mathcal{E}$ is a topos (cf. [Freyd 1987](#freyd87)).
