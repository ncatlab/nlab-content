## Idea

While a [[permutative category]] already induces a [[symmetric spectrum]], [[Brown representability theorem|representing]] the corresponding [[K-theory of a permutative category]], for a [[bipermutative category]] this naturally carries the additional structure of an [[E-infinity ring spectrum]]. The [[generalized (Eilenberg-Steenrod) cohomology]] [[Brown representability theorem|represented]] by this spectrum is called the _([[algebraic K-theory|algebraic]]) [[K-theory]]_ of $C$ and it is hence a [[multiplicative cohomology theory]].
