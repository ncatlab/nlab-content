## Idea

A [[Riemannian manifold]] $(X,g)$ is _conformally flat_ if it is locally taken to a flat manifold by a [[conformal transformation]]; more specifically, if there exists an [[open cover]] $\{U_i \to X\}_{i \in I}$ and on each $U_i$ a [[smooth function]] $f_i$, such that $e^{f_i} g_{\vert U_i}$ has vanishing [[Riemann curvature]]: $R\big( e^{f_i} g_{\vert U_i} \big) = 0$.
 
## Examples

* A [[hyperbolic manifold]] is conformally flat, see [there](hyperbolic+manifold#ConformalFlatness).
