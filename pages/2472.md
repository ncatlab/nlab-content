The rigid analytic geometry is an analogue of the analytic geometry over a nonarchimedean field. The definitions are done by gluing affinoid spaces (see [[affinoid algebra]]) in certain Grothendieck topology. The idea goes back to Tate.

See also [[global analytic geometry]].
