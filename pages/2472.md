The rigid analytic geometry is an analogue of a analytic geometry over a nonarchimedean field. The definitions are done by gluing affinoid spaces (see [[affinoid algebra]]) in certain Grothendieck topology. The idea goes back to Tate.

* Ahmed Abbes, _Éléments de Géométrie Rigide_, vol. I. _Construction et étude géométrique des espaces rigides_, Progress in Mathematics __286__, Birkhäuser 2011, 496 p.[book page](http://perso.univ-rennes1.fr/ahmed.abbes/egr.html)
