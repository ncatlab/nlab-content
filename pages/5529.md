If $a$ is an [[algebraic number]] different from $0$ or $1$, and $b$ [[irrational number|irrational]], then $a^b$ is transcendental (in particular irrational). 

* [[A. O. Gelfond]], _Transcendental and algebraic numbers_, Dover Publications, New York 1960, translation from 1st Russian edition by Leo F. Boron.
