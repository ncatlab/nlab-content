## Idea

In the context of [[topological K-theory]] (at least), *flat K-theory* (first considered in [Karoubi 1987, §7.21](#Karoubi87), [1990, Ex. 3](#Karoubi90)) is the [[Whitehead-generalized cohomology theory]] which is ([[Brown representability theorem|represented by]]) the [[homotopy fiber]] of the [[Chern character]]; equivalently the restriction (namely the [[homotopy pullback]]) of [[differential K-theory]] (any version of it) to vanishing generalized-[[curvature]].

Since the [[coefficients]] of flat [[KU]] form the [[circle group]] $U(1) \simeq \mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$, flat K-theory is also known as *$\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$-K-theory* or as *K-theory with $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$*-coefficients ([Lott 1994](#Lott94)), or similar.
