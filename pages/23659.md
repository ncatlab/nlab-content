## Definition ##

A __premetric space__ is a [[set]] $S$ with a ternary [[relation]] $\sim$ on the [[Cartesian product]] $S \times S \times (0, \infty)$, where $(0, \infty)$ represent the positive [[real numbers]] in $\mathbb{R}$. 

## Properties ##

* Every premetric space is a [[uniform space]]. 

### Premetric topology ###

+-- {: .num_defn #OpenBalls}
###### Definition

Let $(X,\sim^X)$, be a premetric space. Then for every element $x \in X$ and every  $\epsilon \in (0, \infty)$ a [[positive number|positive]] [[real number]], write

$$
  B^\circ_x(\epsilon) 
    \;\coloneqq\;
  \left\{
    y \in X \;\vert\; x \sim_\epsilon y
  \right\}
$$

for the [[open ball]] of [[radius]] $\epsilon$ around $x$.

=--

+-- {: .num_defn #OpenSubsetsOfAMetricSpace}
###### Definition

Let $(X,\sim^X)$ be a premetric space. Say that

1. A _[[neighbourhood]]_ of a point $x \in X$ is a [[subset]] $x \in U \subset X$ which contains some [[open ball]] $B_x^\circ(\epsilon)$ around $x$ (def. \ref{OpenBalls}).

1. An _[[open subset]]_ of $X$ is a [[subset]] $U \subset X$ such that for every for $x \in U$ it also contains a [[neighbourhood]] of $x$.

=--

+-- {: .num_remark}
###### Remark

The collection of open subsets in def. \ref{OpenSubsetsOfAMetricSpace} constitutes a _[[topological space|topology]]_ on the set $X$, making it a _[[topological space]]_. This is called the __premetric topology__. Stated more concisely: the [[open balls]] in a premetric space constitute the [[basis of a topology]] for the premetric topology.

=--


### $\mathbb{Q}_{+}$-premetrics ###

In [[predicative mathematics|predicative]] [[constructive mathematics]], sometimes the positive [[rational numbers]] $\mathbb{Q}_{+}$ are used instead of $(0, \infty)$. We could call these premetrics $\mathbb{Q}_{+}$-premetrics, and the associated spaces $\mathbb{Q}_{+}$-premetric spaces. 

In particular, a $\mathbb{Q}_{+}$-premetric on $\mathbb{R}_H$ is used to define the [[HoTT book real numbers]].
