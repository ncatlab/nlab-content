## Definition

A [[representation]]/[[action]] $V \times G \longrightarrow V$ is _trivial_ if it is given by the [[projection]] out of the [[product]] onto $V$.

## Properties

+-- {: .num_example }
###### Example
**([[induced representation of the trivial representation]])**

Let $G$ be a [[finite group]] and $H \overset{\iota}{\hookrightarrow} G$ a [[subgroup]]-inclusion. Then the [[induced representation]] in [[representation category|Rep(G)]] of the 1-[[dimension|dimensional]] [[trivial representation]] $\mathbf{1} \in Rep(H)$ is the [[permutation representation]] $k[G/H]$ of the [[coset]] [[G-set]] $G/H$:

$$
  \mathrm{ind}_H^G\big( \mathbf{1}\big)
  \;\simeq\;
  k[G/H]
  \,.
$$ 

This follows directly as a special case of the general formula for [[induced representations]] of [[finite groups]] ([this Example](induced+representation#InductionOfFiniteDimensionalRepresentationsOfFiniteGroups)).
