## Idea

The __free group__ on a given [[set]] $S$ is the [[free object]] on $S$ in [[Grp|the category of groups]].  The elements of $S$ are called the __generators__ of this group.

## Definitions

### In type theory

The free group on a set $A$ of [[generators]] is the ([[higher inductive type|higher]]) [[inductive type]] $\mathrm{FreeGroup}(A)$ generated by an element $\mathrm{unit}:\mathrm{FreeGroup}(A)$, a function $\mathrm{gen}:A \to (\mathrm{FreeGroup}(A) \simeq \mathrm{FreeGroup}(A))$, and a [[0-truncation]] constructor $\mathrm{trunc}:\mathrm{isSet}(\mathrm{FreeGroup}(A))$:

## Properties

Every [[subgroup]] of a free group is itself a free group. This is the _[[Nielsen-Schreier theorem]]_.
