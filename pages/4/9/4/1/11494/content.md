## Quotes

> {#TheFirstPoint} The first point is that the enormous usefulness of mathematics in the natural sciences is something bordering on the mysterious and that there is no rational explanation for it. Second, it is just this uncanny usefulness of mathematical concepts that raises the question of the uniqueness of our physical theories. \[...\]

> The statement that the laws of nature are written in the language of mathematics was properly made three hundred years ago [[Galileo Galilei|${}^9$]]; it is now more true than ever before.
