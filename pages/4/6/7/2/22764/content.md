## Definition

\begin{definition}\label{ClosedMidpointAlgebra}
A _closed midpoint algebra_ is a [[cancellative midpoint algebra]] $(M,\vert)$ equipped with a [[pair]] of [[elements]] $\bot, \top \colon  M$. 

In other words, it is a [[bi-pointed object|bi-pointed]] [[cancellative midpoint algebra]]. 
\end{definition}

This Def. \ref{ClosedMidpointAlgebra} may be gleaned from the second half of footnote 28 of [Freyd 2008](#Freyd2008), where it is described as a fragment of the definition of "[[scale]]" given in Section 2 there.

## Properties

\begin{proposition}
  The [[initial object]] among closed midpoint algebras (Def. \ref{ClosedMidpointAlgebra}) is the [[unit interval]] in the [[dyadic rational numbers]].
\end{proposition}
