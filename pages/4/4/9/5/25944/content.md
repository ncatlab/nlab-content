## Definition

A [[topological space]] $(X,\tau)$ is called an **accessible topological space** or a **$T_1$-topological space** if it is both a [[Kolmogorov topological space]] and a [[symmetric topological space]]. Or equivalently, if its [[specialization preorder]] is [[equality]]. Or equivalently still, if all [[singletons]] are closed sets.

## Examples

[[Leibniz's identity of indiscernibles]] implies that every [[set]] $X$ with its [[power set]] $\mathcal{P}(X)$ is an accessible topological space:
$$\left(\forall P \in \mathcal{P}(X).(x \in P) \iff (y \in P)\right) \implies x = y$$
for all $x \in X$ and $y \in X$, since the relation 
$$\forall P \in \mathcal{P}(X).(x \in P) \iff (y \in P)$$
is an [[equivalence relation]].
