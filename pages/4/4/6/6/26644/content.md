## Definition

Let $G = (V,E)$ be a [[simple graph|simple]], [[undirected graph]]. Then $G$ is said to be *complete* as a graph, if for every [[pair]] of [[vertices]] $(v_1,v_2) \in V \times V$, there exists a (unique) [[edge]] $e_{v_1,v_2} \in E$ between them.
