## Idea

The *Hitchin-Thorpe inequality* states the [[Euler characteristic]] is larger than one and a half times the absolute [[signature]] for a [[smooth manifold|smooth]] [[orientable]] [[closed manifold|closed]] [[Einstein manifold|Einstein]] [[4-manifold]]. It therefore serves as an obstruction for the existence of [[Einstein metrics]]. It can be proven using [[Chern-Weil theory]].

## Formulation

For a [[smooth manifold|smooth]] [[orientable]] [[closed manifold|closed]] [[Einstein manifold|Einstein]] [[4-manifold]] $M$, hence with $Ric=\lambda g$ for the [[Ricci curvature]] $Ric$ of its [[Riemannian metric]] $g$ and a scalar $\lambda\in\mathbb{R}$, one has:
$$
\chi(M)
\geq\frac{3}{2}|\sigma(M)|.
$$
