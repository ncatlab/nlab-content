Notion of homotopy behaves bad for locally bad spaces. By Gel'fand-Neimark there is an antiequivalence between the category of unital commutative $C^\ast$-algebras and compact Hausdorff topological spaces. For spaces with bad local behaviour, like quotients, Alain Connes introduced replacements given by noncommutative $C^\ast$-algebras. Hence it is to be expected that in such examples homotopy will be badly behaved. Thus Higson and Connes introduced a notion of [[asymptotic morphism]]s (with nontrivial composition), slightly resembling shape theory of Borsuk. The corresponding version of Kasparov's [[KK-theory]] involving asymptotic morphisms involves so-called E-groups. Restricted to the commutative case E-theory corresponds to certain generalized cohomology theory.
