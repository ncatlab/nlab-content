## Definition

An **$\mathcal{A}$-monoid** or **affine monoid** or **antithesis monoid** is an [[A-set|$\mathcal{A}$-set]] $M$ with an [[A-function|$\mathcal{A}$-function]] $m:M \otimes M \to M$ from the [[tensor product of A-sets|tensor product]] $M \otimes M$ to $M$ and an element $e$ in $M$ such that $(M, \sim, e, m)$ is a [[monoidal setoid]]. 

An $\mathcal{A}$-monoid $M$ is **strong** if $m$ is instead a function from the [[cartesian product of A-sets|cartesian product]] $M \times M$ to $M$. 

An $\mathcal{A}$-monoid $M$ is **commutative** if $(M, \sim, e, m)$ is a [[braided monoidal setoid]].
