## Idea

The analogue of [[quaternionic structure]] for [[para-quaternions]].


## Definition

### Para-quaternionic structure on vector spaces
 {#ComplexStructureOnAVectorSpace}
\begin{definition}
\label{DefParaQuaternionicStructure}
A **para-quaternionic structure** on a [[vector space]] $V$ is a [[Lie algebra|Lie subalgebra]] $Q\subset \text{End}(V)$ of the [[endomorphism Lie algebra]] which admits a [[linear basis]] $(J_1, J_2, J_3)$ such that $J_3 = J_1 J_2$ and $J^2_{\alpha}= \epsilon_{\alpha} Id$, where $(\epsilon_1,\epsilon_2,\epsilon_3)=(-1,1,1)$.
\end{definition}

### Para-quaternionic Kähler manifold

\begin{definition}
A [[pseudo-Riemannian manifold]] $(M, g)$ of dimension $\geq 5$ endowed with a parallel [[distribution of subspaces|distribution]] $Q_p\subset \text{End}(T_p M)$ of $g$-[[skew-symmetric matrix|skew-symmetric]] para-quaternionic structures is called a **para-quaternionic Kähler manifold**.
\end{definition}

The metric $g$ of a para-quaternionic Kähler manifold has signature $(2n, 2n)$ and is [[Einstein manifold|Einstein]].
