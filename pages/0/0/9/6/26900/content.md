## Idea

A useful smoothness criterium for [[noncommutative algebra|noncommutative]] [[graded algebras]].

## Definition

Let $k$ be a [[field]] and $A$ a connected [[finitely generated algebra|finitely generated]] [[associative algebra|associative]] [[natural numbers|$\mathbb{N}$]]-[[graded algebra|graded]] $k$-algebra. 

$A$ is __Artin-Schelter regular__ (AS regular) if 
the following three conditions hold:

1. it is of [[finite number|finite]] [[global dimension]] $d$

2. has polynomial growth ($dim A_n$ is bounded above by a [[polynomial function]] $f(n)$)

3. is [[Gorenstein ring|Gorenstein]]: $Ext_A^i({}_A k,A) = 0$ for $i\neq d$ and $Ext_A^d({}_A k,A) = k_A$.

## Literature

The notion was  introduced  (under the name *regular algebra*) in :

* [[Michael Artin]], W. F. Schelter, _Graded algebras of global dimension 3_, Adv. Math. __66__ 2 (1987) 171-216 \[<a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/0001-8708(87)90034-X">doi:10.1016/0001-8708(87)90034-X</a>\]
