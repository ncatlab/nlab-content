## Idea

An [[EI (∞,1)-category]] is an [[(∞,1)-category]] in which every [[endomorphism]] is an [[equivalence in an (∞,1)-category|equivalence]]. This induces a [[preorder|ordering]] $\prec$ on the [[equivalence classes]] of [[objects]], where x≺y means that there is a noninvertible [[morphism]] $y \to x$. 

An **inverse EI (∞,1)-category** is an EI (∞,1)-category which is also an [[inverse category|inverse]] (∞,1)-category, that is, is such that $\prec$ is [[well-founded relation|well-founded]], i.e., there are no infinite chains of noninvertible morphisms $ \to \to \to \cdots$.

## Examples

* For a [[compact Lie group]] $G$ the opposite of the [[orbit category]].
* The category of [[finite sets]] and [[surjective functions]].
* An EI (∞,1)-category with [[finite number|finitely many]] objects.
