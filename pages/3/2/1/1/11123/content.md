## Idea

The definition of [[pure motive|pure]]/[[mixed motive|mixed]] [[motive]] as a [[correspondence]] (of suitable [[smooth schemes]]) equipped with an [[algebraic cycle]] on its tip may be generalized to that of a correspondence carrying instead a cycle in [[algebraic K-theory]] ([Gillet-Soul&#233; 96](#GilletSoule96), [Gillet-Soul&#233; 09](#GilletSoule09)). 

These might be called _Gillet-Soul&#233; motives_ ([Tabuada 13, section 5.3](#Tabuada13)) or just _K-motives_.

Via the algebraic [[Chern character]] these K-motives map to rational pure [[Chow motives]]  ([Tabuada 13, p. 9](#Tabuada13)). Moreover, they faithfully embed into [[noncommutative motives]] ([Tabuada 13, p. 10](#Tabuada13)).
