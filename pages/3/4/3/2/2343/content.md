#Motivation#

The construction of moduli spaces depends strongly on local properties like smoothness and transversality of intersections when trying to represent the functor of assigning families of objects to the varying base of family. When passing to the classes of equivalent objects, one faces the problem of having nontrivial automorphisms. At the infinitesimal level automorphisms correspond to the derivations. Taking derivations is represented by the module of relative [[KÃ¤hler differential]]s which sufficies in good cases. Its correct [[derived algebraic geometry|derived]] replacement is the cotangent complex of Grothendieck-Illusie.
