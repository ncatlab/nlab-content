## Definition

\begin{definition}
A [[G-∞-category]] $\mathcal{C}$ is **G-semiadditive** if, for all [[finite set|finite]] [[G-sets|H-sets]] $S$ and [[tuples]] $(X_{H_i} \in \mathcal{C}_{H_i})_{G/H_i \in \mathrm{Orb}(S)}$, the canonical [[map]] from the [[indexed coproduct]] to the [[indexed product]]
$$
  \coprod_{H_i}^G X_{H_i} \rightarrow \prod_{H_i}^G X_{H_i}
$$
is an [[equivalence in an (infinity,1)-category|equivalence]].

More generally, if $I$ is a [[indexing system|weak indexing category]], we say that $\mathcal{C}$ is **$I$-semiadditive** if for all $I$-admissible [[G-set|H-sets]] $S$ and tuples $(X_{H_i} \in \mathcal{C}_{H_i})_{G/H_i \in \mathrm{Orb}(S)}$, the canonical map
$$
  \coprod_{H_i}^G X_{H_i} \rightarrow \prod_{H_i}^G X_{H_i}
$$
is an [[equivalence in an (infinity,1)-category|equivalence]].
\end{definition}
