## Embedding of the gauge group of the standard model

$SU(5)$ is of particular importence for the [[standard model of particle physics#GaugeGroup|gauge group of the standard model]]. It is composed of the:

* first [[unitary group]] $U(1)$ for the electromagnetic interaction described by [[quantum electrodynamics]] (QED). $dim U(1)=1$ corresponds to the one photon.
* second [[special unitary group]] $SU(2)$ for the [[weak interaction]] described by quantum flavordynamics (QFD). $dim SU(2)=3$ corresponds to the three massive [[gauge bosons]] $W^-$, $W^+$ and $Z$.
* third [[special unitary group]] $SU(3)$ for the [[strong interaction]] described by [[quantum chromodynamics]] (QCD). $dim SU(3)=8$ corresponds to the eight types of [[gluons]].

It is not the product $U(1)\times SU(2)\times SU(3)$, but instead its [[quotient group]] $G_SM=(U(1)\times SU(2)\times SU(3))/\mathbb{Z}_6$ (with all known particles invariant under this $\mathbb{Z}_6$ action). There is an embedding:
$$
\Phi\colon
U(1)\times SU(2)\times SU(3)\hookrightarrow SU(5),
(e^{i\varphi},U,V)\mapsto\begin{pmatrix}
e^{-3i\varphi}U & 0 \\
0 & e^{2i\varphi}V
\end{pmatrix}
$$
It has $ker(\Phi)\cong\mathbb{Z}_6$ generated by $(\zeta_6,\zeta_3\mathbf{1}_2,\zeta_2\mathbf{1}_3)$ with the [[primitive roots of unity]] $\zeta_6,\zeta_2=\zeta_6^3,\zeta_3=\zeta_6^2\in U(1)$. Hence $G_SM\cong img(\Phi)\cong S(U(2)\times U(3))$.
