## Idea

What is called *compact QED* (following [Polyakov 1975](#Polyakov1975), [1977](#Polyakov1977), [1987 §4.3](#Polyakov1987)) is [[quantum electrodynamics]] (QED) with the [[compact topological group|compactness]] of the [[circle group|$U(1)$]] [[gauge group]] taken properly into account (which is not typically done in [[perturbative quantum field theory|perturbation theory]]). 

> The pleonastic terminology results from the infamous tradition in parts of [[theoretical physics]] to conflate [[Lie groups]] --- here the [[compact topological group|compact]] [[circle group|$U(1)$]] or the non-compact $\mathbb{R}$ --- , with their [[Lie algebras]], which here in both cases is $\mathfrak{u}(1) \simeq \mathbb{R}$. After this conflation, people have to speak of a "compact $U(1)$" to indicate that they really mean the [[circle group]], cf. [Preskill 1984 p. 471](monopole#Preskill1984).

In particular, in the context of "compact QED" one considers [[magnetic monopole]] [[background field|backgrounds]] and [[condensates]] which in 3 dimensions are argued ([Polyakov 1975](#Polyakov1975), [1977](#Polyakov1977), [1997](#Polyakov1997)) to exhibit [[confinement]] in an [[abelian group|abelian]] version of the [[dual superconductor model of color confinement]].
