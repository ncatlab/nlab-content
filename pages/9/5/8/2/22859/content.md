Differential linear logic was introduced by [[Thomas Ehrhard]] and [[Laurent Regnier]] to be  a syntactic proof-theoretic approach to differential calculus, then differential categories were developed by [[Blute]], [[Cockett]] and [[Seely]], to provide a categorical counterpart for these ideas.
