## Idea

In [[simplicial type theory]] and related approaches to [[synthetic (infinity,1)-category theory]], the [[twisted arrow construction]] is given by a [[modality]] $(-)^\mathrm{tw}$. Unlike the [[twisted arrow construction]] in the usual [[analytic versus synthetic|analytic]] approaches to [[(infinity,1)-category theory]] like [[quasicategories]] or [[complete Segal spaces]], the twisted arrow modality can be applied to any type by definition of modality, not just merely the [[complete Segal types]]. 

Given a [[type]] $A$, the $n$-simplicies in $A^\mathrm{tw}$ are [[equivalence of types|equivalent]] to the $(2n + 1)$-simplicies in $A$. 

The twisted arrow modality is used in defining the [[(infinity,1)-Yoneda embedding]].
