## Idea

In [[quantum mechanics]], the *uncertainty principle* states that the best possible experimental uncertainties of [[expectation values]] of a pair of [[quantum observable|quantum]] [[observables]] whose [[commutator]] is a non-vanishing multiple of the identity operator are complementary: To the extent that the uncertainty in one observable shrinks, the uncertainy in the other observable has to grow. In particular it is impossible to know both observables jointly with certainty.

(But see [Uffink & Hilgevoord 1985](#UffinkHilgevoord1985) for a more careful statement of the situation.)
