### In solid state physics

Under the expected [[K-theory classification of topological phases of matter]], *time-reversal symmetry* refers to that sector of [[twisted equivariant K-theory]] which is [[KR-theory]], where the [[cyclic group of order two|$\mathbb{Z}/2$]] $\simeq \{\mathrm{E}, T\}$ acts 

* on the [[Brillouin torus]] by [[momentum]]-reversal $T \,\colon\,k \mapstp -k$

*and*

* on the [[Hamiltonian]] and its related [[observables]] (specifically: the [[Fredholm operators]] $F$ whose space classifies K-theory) by [[complex conjugation]] $T \colon F \mapsto \overline{F}$.
