# Homological category
* table of contents
{: toc}

## Definition

A category $C$ is __homological__ if it is 

1. [[pointed category|pointed]]

1. [[regular category|regular]] 

1. [[protomodular category|protomodular]].

## Properties

Many of the standard results of classical [[homological algebra]] in abelian categories extend to homological categories: 

* the [[five lemma]] 

* the [[nine lemma]]

* the [[snake lemma]] 

* the [[long exact sequence in homology]]

* the Noether isomorphism theorem 

* a version of the [[Jordan-HÃ¶lder theorem]].  

A homological category which is [[Barr-exact]] and has finite [[coproducts]] is [[semiabelian category|semiabelian]].

## Examples

\begin{example}\label{CategoryOfGroupsIsHomological}
 The category [[Grp]] of all [[groups]] (including [[non-abelian groups]]) is homological. Namely it is 

1. regular, by [this example](regular+category#CategoryOfGroupsIsRegular),

1. pointed protomodular by [this example](protomodular+category#GroupsFormAProtomodularCategory).

\end{example}
