## Idea

An _$n$-topos_ is an [[n-category]] analog of a [[topos]].

An $n$-topos that is an [[(n,1)-category]], hence where all [[k-morphism]]s for $k \geq 2$ are [[equivalence in an (infinity,1)-category|equivalences]] is called an [[(n,1)-topos]]. See there for more.

## Examples 

For every $n$, The canonical $(n+1)$-topos is [[nCat]], the [[n-category|(n+1)-category]] of [[n-categories]].
