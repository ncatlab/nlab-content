__J. Barkley Rosser__ (1907-1989) was an American mathematical logician, a former PhD student of [[Alonzo Church]].

* wikipedia [J. Barkley Rosser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._Barkley_Rosser)

He is known for Church-Rosser theorem ([wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church%E2%80%93Rosser_theorem)) in $\lambda$-[[lambda calculus|calculus]], Rosser sieve in number theory, Rosser's trick (a strengthening of the Goedel's incompleteness theorem, [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosser%27s_trick)) and the  Kleene-Rosser paradox  ([wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kleene%E2%80%93Rosser_paradox)).

He wrote the monograph

* J. Barkley Rosser, _Logic for mathematicians_, 1954

where we can also find a formalization of [[rule C]].
