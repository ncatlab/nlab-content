## Idea

By [[Bloch-Floquet theory]] the available [[energies]] of [[electron]]-excitations in [[crystals]] depends smoothly on the [[momentum]]/[[wave number]] $k$ of the excitation, taking values in the [[Brillouin torus]], and otherwise only on a [[discrete space|discrete]] index set labelling the remaining "[[quantum numbers]]" (the [[electron]]'s [[orbitals]], [[spin]]. etc.). Fixing the latter values (and jointly denoting them $\mathbf{n}$, say), the given function $k \mapsto E_{\mathbf{n}}(k)$ is called the *$\mathbf{n}$th electronic band* (or just *$\mathbf{n}$th band*, for short) of the material.
