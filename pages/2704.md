## Idea

*Specht modules* are [[linear representations]] of the [[symmetric group]] $Sym(n)$ (for any $ \in \mathbb{N}$), hence [[modules]] over the [[group ring]] $k(Sym(n))$, which are indexed by the [[partitions]] of $n$. In [[characteristic 0]], they are [[irreducible representations|irreducible]] and exhaust the [[isomorphism classes]] of [[irreps]] (e.g. [Sagan 01, Thm. 2.4.6](#Sagan01)). 

Over a field of [[positive characteristic]] $p$, where $p \mid n!$, the Specht modules are not irreducible, but every [[irreducible module]] does appear as the [[cosocle]] of a Specht module.
