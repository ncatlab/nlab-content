## Idea

In an appendix of [[SGA4]] (vol. II,ch.VI, p.336) [[Pierre Deligne]] proved a theorem concerning the _points_ of a [[coherent topos]]. As points of a topos $\mathcal{E}$ correspond to the models of the theory classified by $\mathcal{E}$ this result can be interpreted from a logical perspective where it basically amounts to a variant of the classical _Gödel-Henkin completeness theorem_ for [[first-order logic]] as observed by [[William Lawvere]] (1975).

+-- {: .num_theorem }
###Theorem

A [[coherent topos]] has [[enough points]].

=--

As a corollary of the [[Deligne-Lurie completeness theorem]] this appears as ([[Spectral Schemes|Lurie SpecSchm, corollary 4.2]]).
