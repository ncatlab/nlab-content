The Lefschetz hyperplane section theorem says that [[cohomology|cohomologically]] a nonsingular [[complex variety]] looks like its [[hyperplane]] sections. More precisely, 

+-- {: .un_theorem}
###### Hyperplane section theorem
Let $X$ be an [[algebraic variety|algebraic subvariety]] of complex projective space and $H$ a generic hyperplane in $\mathbb{C}^n$.
Then the $i$-th [[relative cohomology]] $H_i(X,X\cap H) = 0$ for $i\lt n$. 
=--

There is also a version of the quantum hyperplane section theorem due to Y.-P. Lee, where the cohomology is replaced by the quantum cohomology.
