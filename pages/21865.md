[[formal grammar|Formal grammars]] come in a hierarchy of expressive power, first introduced by [[Noam Chomsky]].

* **Type-0 grammars**, also known as [[formal grammar|unrestricted grammars]] generate the [[decidability|recursively enumerable]] languages, they correspond to [[Turing machine|Turing machines]].
* **Type-1 grammars** are also called [[context sensitive grammar|context sensitive grammars]], they correspond to linear-bounded [[nondeterministic automaton |nondeterministic]] Turing machines.
* **Type-2 grammars** are also called [[context free grammar|context free grammars]], they correspond to [[nondeterministic automaton |nondeterministic]] pushdown [[automaton|automata]].
* **Type-3 grammars** generate regular languages, they are equivalent to [[regular expression|regular expressions]] and finite-state [[automaton|automata]].
