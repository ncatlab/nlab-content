## Definition

Given a unital [[ring]] $k$, a _monic polynomial_ over $k$ is a [[polynomial]] with [[coefficients]] in $k$, whose highest order coefficient is $1$. 

A [[root]] of a monic polynomial over $k$ is by definition an _[[algebraic integer]]_ over $k$. 

Here _algebraic integer_ usually means _algebraic integer over $\mathbf{Z}$_. All algebraic integers form a [[field]] called the _[[integral closure]]_ of $\mathbf{Z}$ in $\mathbf{C}$. 

On the other hand, for a [[number field]] $K$, an [[integer]] in $K$ is an algebraic integer over $\mathbf{Z}$ which is in $K$; all integers in $K$ form a __ring of integers__ $\mathcal{O}_K$ of the number field $K$.
