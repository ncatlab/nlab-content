## Definition

For $n \in \mathbb{N}$ write $B^n \mathbb{Z}_2$ for the [[classifying space]] of [[ordinary cohomology]] in degree $n$ with coefficients in $\mathbb{Z}_2$, regarded as an object in the [[homotopy category]] $H$ [[model structure on topological spaces|of topological spaces]].

Notice that for $X$ any topological space ([[CW-complex]]), 

$$
  H^n(X, \mathbb{Z}_2) := H(X, B^n \mathbb{Z}_2) 
$$

is the [[ordinary cohomology]] of $X$ in degree $n$ with coefficients in $\mathbb{Z}_2$. Therefore, by the [[Yoneda lemma]], [[natural transformations]]

$$
  H^{k}(-, \mathbb{Z}_2) \to H^l(-, \mathbb{Z}_2)
$$

correspond bijectively to morphisms $B^k \mathb{Z}_2 \to B^l \mathbb{Z}_2$.

The following characterization is due to ([SteenrodEpstein](#SteenrodEpstein)).

+-- {: .num_defn}
###### Definition

The **Steenrod squares** are a family of morphisms

$$
  Sq^n : B^k \mathbb{Z}_2 \to B^{k + n} \mathbb{Z}_2
$$

for all $n,k \in \mathbb{N}$ satisfying the following conditions

1. for $n = 0$ it is the [[identity]];

1. if $X$ is a [[manifold]] of [[dimension]] $dim X \lt n$ then $Sq^n = 0$;

1. for $k = n$ the morphism $Sq^n : B^n \mathbb{Z}_2 \to B^{2n} \mathbb{Z}_2$ is the [[cup product]] $x \mapsto x \cup x$;

1. $Sq^n(x \cup y) = \sum_{i + j = n} (Sq^i x) \cup (Sq^j y)$;


=--

An analogous definition works for coefficients in $\mathbb{Z}_p$ for any $p \gt 2$. The corresponding oerations are usually denoted

$$
  P^n : B^k \mathbb{Z}_p \to B^{k+n} \mathbb{Z}_{p}
  \,.
$$

## Properties

### Relation to Bockstein homomorphism

$Sq^1$ is the [[Bockstein homomorphism]] of the [[short exact sequence]] $\mathbb{Z}_2 \to \mathbb{Z}_4 \to \mathbb{Z}_2$.

### Adem relations

$$
  Sq^i \circ Sq^j 
   = 
  \sum_{k = 0}^{[i/2]}
  \left(
    \array{
      j - k - 1
      \\
      i - 2k 
    }
  \right)_{mod 2}
  Sq^{i + j -k} \circ Sq^k
$$

for all $0 \lt i \lt 2 j$.
