
In one direction, assume that $V(\mathcal{F})$ is irreducible, and that $f,g \in R$ with $f \cdot g \in \mathcal{F}$. We need to show that then already $f \in \mathcal{F}$ or $g \in \mathcal{F}$. 

Observe that $V(\mathcal{F}) \subset V((f)) \cup V((g))$. This is because 

$$
  \begin{aligned}
    & p \in V(\mathcal{F})
    \\
    \Leftrightarrow\;
    &
    \mathcal{F} \subset p
    \\
    \Rightarrow \;
    &
    f \cdot g \in p
    \\
    \Rightarrow\;
    &
    \left(
      f \in p
    \right)
    \,\text{or}\,
    \left(
      g \in p
    \right)
    \\
    \Leftrightarrow\;
    &
    \left(
      p \in V(g)
    \right)
    \,\text{or}\,
    \left(
      p \in V(f) 
    \right)
    \\
    \Leftrightarrow\;
    &
    p \in V(f) \cup V(g)
  \end{aligned}
$$
