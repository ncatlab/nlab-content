## Introduction

We give a proof that [[groupoid | groupoids]] model [[homotopy 1-type | homotopy 1-types]]. More precisely, we construct a Quillen equivalence between a model structure on the category of groupoids, and a certain model structure on the category of cubical sets.

## Preliminaries

+-- {: .num_defn}
###### Notation

We denote the category of [[groupoid | groupoids]] by $\mathsf{Grpd}$.

=--

+-- {: .num_defn}
###### Notation

We make use throughout of the notation of [[category of cubes]], [[cubical set]], [[cubical truncation, skeleton, and co-skeleton]], and [[fundamental groupoid of a cubical set]]. 

=--

## One direction of the equivalence.

+-- {: .num_defn}
###### Proposition

The adjunction natural transformation $\epsilon : \Pi_{1} \circ N \rightarrow id$ is a natural isomorphism.

=--

+-- {: .proof}
###### Proof

Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a groupoid. Let $r(\mathcal{A}) : \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \Pi_{1} \circ N$ be the functor defined as follows.

1) On objects it is the identity.

2) To an arrow $f : x_{0} \rightarrow x_{1}$ of $\mathcal{A}$, we associate the arrow   

$$
   \array{
      x_{0}  &  \overset{f}{\rightarrow} & x_{1} \overset{id}{\leftarrow} & x_{1} 
   }
$$

of $\Pi_{1} \circ N(\mathcal{A})$.

Because of 1 b) ii) of Notation \ref{NotationFundamentalGroupoid}, the following diagram of commutative squares illustrates that $r(g \circ f) = r(g) \circ r(f)$, where $f : x_{0} \rightarrow x_{1}$ and $g : x_{1} \rightarrow x_{2}$ are arrows of $\mathcal{A}$. 

$$
   \array{
      x_{0}         & \overset{f}{\rightarrow}          & x_{1}        & \overset{id}{\rightarrow} & x_{1}        & \overset{g}{\rightarrow}   & x_{2}         & \overset{id}{\leftarrow} & x_{2} \\
      id \downarrow &                                   & \downarrow g &                           & \downarrow g &                            & \downarrow id &                          & \downarrow id \\
      x_{0}         & \underset{g \circ f}{\rightarrow} & x_{2}        & \underset{id}{\leftarrow} & x_{2}        & \underset{id}{\rightarrow} & x_{2}         & \underset{id}{\leftarrow} & x_{2}
   }
$$

That $r(id) = id$ follows immediately from 1 b) i) of Notation \ref{NotationFundamentalGroupoid}.

It is clear that $\epsilon(\mathcal{A}) \circ r(\mathcal{A})$ is $id(\mathcal{A})$.

Moreover, because of 1 b) ii) of Notation \ref{NotationFundamentalGroupoid}, the following diagram of commutative squares illustrates that $r(\mathcal{A}) \circ \epsilon(\mathcal{A})$ is $id(\mathcal{A})$. 

$$
   \array{
      x_{0}         & \overset{f_{1}}{\rightarrow} & x_{1}        & \overset{g_{1}}{\leftarrow} & x_{2}        & \cdots & x_{2n-2}     & \overset{f_{n}}{\rightarrow} & x_{2n-1}              & \overset{g_{n}}{\leftarrow} & x_{2n} \\
      id \downarrow &                              & \downarrow A &                              & \downarrow B &        & \downarrow C &                              & \downarrow g_{n}^{-1} &                             & \downarrow id \\
      x_{0}         & \underset{D}{\rightarrow}    & x_{2n}       & \underset{id}{\leftarrow} & x_{2n}          & \cdots & x_{2n}       & \underset{id}{\rightarrow} & x_{2n}                  & \underset{id}{\leftarrow}   & x_{2n}
   }
$$

Here 

$$
   \array{
      x_{0}  &  \overset{f_{1}}{\rightarrow} & x_{1} \overset{g_{1}}{\leftarrow} & x_{2} & \cdots & x_{2n-2} & \overset{f_{n}}{\rightarrow} & x_{2n-1} \overset{g_{n}}{\leftarrow} & x_{2n}
   }
$$

is any zig-zag of arrows of $\mathcal{A}$, and the arrows A, B, C, and D are given as follows.

A) $g_{n}^{-1} \circ f_{n} \circ \cdots g_{2}^{-1} \circ f_{2} \circ g_{1}^{-1}$

B) $g_{n}^{-1} \circ f_{n} \circ \cdots g_{2}^{-1} \circ f_{2}$

C) $g_{n}^{-1} \circ f_{n}$

D) $g_{n}^{-1} \circ f_{n} \circ \cdots g_{2}^{-1} \circ f_{2} \circ g_{1}^{-1} \circ f_{1}$

We also make use of 1 b) i) of Notation \ref{NotationFundamentalGroupoid} above in identifying 

$$
    \array{x_{0} & \underset{D}{\rightarrow} & x_{2n} & \underset{id}{\leftarrow} & x_{2n} & \cdots & x_{2n} & \underset{id}{\rightarrow} & x_{2n} & \underset{id}{\leftarrow} & x_{2n}}
$$

with 

$$
    \array{x_{0} & \underset{D}{\rightarrow} & x_{2n} & \underset{id}{\leftarrow} x_{2n}}
$$

as required.
