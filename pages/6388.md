## Idea

What are called _Einstein's equations_ are the [[equations of motion]] of gravity: the [[Euler-Lagrange equations]] induced by the [[Einstein-Hilbert action]].

They say that the [[Einstein tensor]] $G$ of the [[metric]]/the [[field (physics)|field]] of [[gravity]] [[equality|equals]] the [[energy-momentum tensor]] $T$ of the remaining [[force]]- and [[matter]]-[[field (physics)|fields]]:

$$
  G = T
  \,.
$$

## Properties

### Existence and uniqueness

Given a choice of [[Cauchy surface]] $\Sigma$, the [[initial value problem]] for Einstein's [[differential equations]] of motion is determined by a choice of [[Riemannian metric]] on $\Sigma$ and a second fundamental form along $\Sigma$.

With this data a solution to the equation exists and is unique. ([Klainerman-Nicolo 03](#KlainermanNicolo03)).

* [[Sergiu Klainerman]], Francesco Nicolo, _The evolution problem in general relativity_, Progress in Mathematical Physics, 25. Birkhäuser Boston, Inc., Boston, MA, 2003. xiv+385 pp. ISBN: 0-8176-4254-4
