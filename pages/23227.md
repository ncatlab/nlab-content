## Idea

In [[group theory]], *Lagrange's theorem* asserts that for $G$ a [[finite group]] and $H \,\subset\, G$ a [[subgroup]], the [[order of a group|order]] $\left\vert H\right\vert$ of $H$ divides the order $\left\vert G \right\vert$ of $G$, in that the [[quotient]]

$$
  [G : H]
  \;\coloneqq\;
  \frac
    { \left\vert G \right\vert }
    { \left\vert H \right\vert }
  \;\;\;
  \in
  \;
  \mathbb{N}
$$

is a [[natural number]], called the [[index of a subgroup|index]] of $H$ in $G$.
