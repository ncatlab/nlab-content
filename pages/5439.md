## Idea

A Hilbert $C^\ast$-module (or simply a Hilbert module) is a generalization of Hilbert space where the inner product is allowed to be valued in the $C^\ast$-algebra. 

## Definition

Let $A$ be a complex $C^\ast$-algebra. A complex [[vector space]] $E$ which is in compatible way a (say left) $A$-module has a structure of a __Hilbert $C^\ast$-module__ over $A$ if it is given an inner product, i.e. a sesquilinear map (that is left antilinear in the first and right linear in the second argument) $\langle - , - \rangle : E\times E\to A$ which is conjugate symmetric ($\langle a,b\rangle = \langle b,a\rangle^*$ for all $a,b\in E$) and positive definite (for all $e\in E$, $\langle e,e\rangle \geq 0$ in the sense of the positivity of elements in the $C^\ast$-algebra $A$ and $\langle e,e\rangle = 0$ only if $x = 0$).
