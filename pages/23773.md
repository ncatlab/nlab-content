##Â Definition ##

For a [[commutative ring]] $R$, let $R[x]$ be the [[free object|free]] [[commutative algebra|commutative $R$-algebra]] on the singleton $\mathbb{1}$ with element $\bullet \in \mathbb{1}$, with canonical function $x:\mathbb{1} \to r$, and let $R \to R$ be the [[function algebra]] of $R$, with [[identity function]] $id_R:R \to R$. Since both objects are commutative $R$-algebras there are canonical commutative ring [[homomorphisms]] $\alpha:R \to R[x]$ and $\beta:R \to (R \to R)$, and there exists a function $i:R[x] \to (R \to R)$ such that $i \circ \alpha = \beta$ and $i(x(\bullet)) = id_R$. A **polynomial function** is a function $f:R \to R$ in the [[image]] of $i:R[x] \to (R \to R)$.
