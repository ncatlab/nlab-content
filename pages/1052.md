Let $D$ be a small category and $C$ a category, and $d:D\to C$ a diagram. A __cocone__ (or inductive cone) over $d$ is a a pair $(e,u)$ of an object $e\in C$ and a natural transformation $u : d\to \Delta e$ (where $\Delta e$ is the constant diagram $\Delta e:D\to C$, $x\mapsto e$, $x\in D$). 

A __morphism of cocones__ $(e,u)\mapsto (e',u')$ is a morphism $\gamma:e\to e'$ in $C$ such that $\gamma\circ u_x=u'_x$ for all objects $x$ in $D$ (symbollically $(\Delta \gamma)\circ u = u'$); the composition being the composition of underlying morphisms in $C$. Thus cocones form a category whose initial object if it exists is a [[colimit]] of $d$.
