## Idea

The _nuclear matrix model_ ([Hashimoto-Iizuka-Yi 10](#HashimotoIizukaYi10), [Hashimoto-Matsuo-Morita 19](#HashimotoMatsuoMorita19)) is a [[matrix model]] for [[baryons]]/[[nucleons]] in [[nuclear physics]].

The model proceeds from the [[Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model]] for [[QCD]] realized on [[intersecting D-brane model|intersecting D-branes]], where [[baryons]] are embodied by [[wrapped brane|wrapped]] [[D4-branes]] ("[Witten's baryon vertex](AdS-QCD+correspondence#Baryons)") on [[flavour brane|flavour]] [[D8-branes]] ([[D4-D8 brane bound states]]). 

<center>
<img src="https://ncatlab.org/nlab/files/WSSBraneConfigurationEngineeringQCDII.jpg" width="640"/>
</center>

> (graphics from [[schreiber:Differential Cohomotopy implies intersecting brane observables|Sati-Schreiber 19c]])

Thus, encoding the [[D-brane]] dynamics of these [[D4-branes]] in a matrix model along the lines of the [[BFSS matrix model]]/[[BMN matrix model]] (for [[D0-branes]]) and the [[IKKT matrix model]] (for [[D(-1)-branes]]) leads to a matrix model for [[baryons]] and hence, potentially, for [[nucleons]].
