#Definition#

The Clifford algebra $Cl(V)$ for $V$ a real [[inner product space]] of dimension $d$ is the quotient

$$
  Cl(V) := T(V) / (v \cdot v + |v|^2 \cdots Id)
$$

of the [[tensor algebra]] $T(V)$

$$
  T(V) = \mathbb{R} \oplus V \oplus (V \otimes V)
     \oplus (V \otimes V \otimes V) \oplus \cdots
$$

It's dimension is $dim(Cl(V)) = 2^d$

#As a superalgebra#

The Clifford algebra t is naturally a [[super algebra]]

$$
  Cl(V) = Cl(V)^{ev} \oplus Cl(V)^{odd}
$$

#Relation to $Spin$ group#

Write $Cl(V)^\times$ for the [[group of units]] of the Clifford algebra $Cl(V)$: the group of all elements in $Cl(V)$ that have an [[inverse]] under the product in the algebra.

The [[Spin group]] on $V$ is a subgroup of $Cl(V)^\times$.

First of all, the [[Pin group]] $Pin(V) \subset Cl(V)^\times$ is the group generated by the elements that have unit norm, $|v| = 1$.

The [[Spin group]] is the intersection of the [[Pin group]] with the even part of the Clifford algebra:

$$
  \array{
    Spin(V) := & Pin(V) \cap Cl(V)^{ev} &\to&
      Pin(V)
    \\
    & \downarrow^{double cov.} && \downarrow^{double cov.}
    \\
    & SO(V) &\hookrightarrow& O(V)
  }
$$ 

#Examples#

In very low dimension the Clifford algebras are the following:


$$
  \array{
    d & Cl(V)^{ev} & Spin(V)
    \\
    \\
    0 & \mathbb{R} & \{\pm 1\}
    \\
    1 & \mathbb{R} & \{\pm 1\}
    \\
    2 & \{a \cdot 1 + b e_1 e_2\}
        \simeq \mathbb{C}
     & S^1 = U(1)
   }
$$

where $\{e_1, e_2\}$ is the canonical basis of $\mathbb{R}^2 \simeq V$.
