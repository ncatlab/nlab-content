
### Matching KN black holes to elementary particles
 {#ReferencesMatchingKNBlackHolesToElementaryParticles}

On the similarity between [[Kerr-Newman black holes]] (charged and spinning "[[black brane|black 0-branes]]") and [[elementary particles]] ([[super p-brane|sigma-model 0-branes]]) like [[electrons]].

Historical precursor discussion on the possibility of *[[geometrodynamics]]* for [[fundamental particles]]:

* {#EinsteinInfeldHoffmann39} [[Albert Einstein]], [[Leopold Infeld]], B. Hoffmann, *The gravitational equations and the problem of motion*, Annals of Mathematics **39** 1 (1938) \[<a href="https://doi.org/10.2307/1968714">doi:10.2307/1968714</a>, [jstor:1968714](https://www.jstor.org/stable/1968714)\]

Seminal observation that the gyromagnetic ratio of the [[Kerr-Newman black hole]] is 2, just as the for [[electron]] (cf. at *[[anomalous magnetic moment]]*):

* Brandon Carter, p. 1562 of: *Global Structure of the Kerr Family of Gravitational Fields*, Phys. Rev. **174** (1968) 1559 \[<a href="https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRev.174.1559">doi:10.1103/PhysRev.174.1559</a>\]

* Werner Israel, *Source of the Kerr Metric*, Phys. Rev. D **2** 641 (1970) \[<a href="https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.2.641">doi:10.1103/PhysRevD.2.641</a>\]

Further discussion:

* Carlos A. López, *Extended model of the electron in general relativity*, Phys. Rev. D **30** 313 (1984) \[<a href="https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.30.313">doi:10.1103/PhysRevD.30.313<a>\]

* H. I. Arcos, J. G. Pereira: *Kerr–Newman Solution as a Dirac Particle*, General Relativity and Gravitation **36** (2004) 2441–2464 \[<a href="https://doi.org/10.1023/B:GERG.0000046832.71368.a5">doi:10.1023/B:GERG.0000046832.71368.a5</a>, [arXiv:hep-th/0210103](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/0210103)\] 

* Alexander Burinskii, *The Dirac-Kerr-Newman electron*, Gravit. Cosmol. **14** (2008) 109–122 \[<a href="https://doi.org/10.1134/S0202289308020011">doi:10.1134/S0202289308020011</a>, [arXiv:hep-th/0507109](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/0507109)\]

* Alexander Burinskii, *Kerr–Newman electron as spinning soliton*,  International Journal of Modern Physics A **29** 26, 1450133 (2014)  \[<a href="https://doi.org/10.1142/S0217751X14501334">doi:10.1142/S0217751X14501334</a>, [arXiv:1410.2888](https://arxiv.org/abs/1410.2888)\]
