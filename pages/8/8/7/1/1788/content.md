## Idea

It is known since ... that limits of [[open string]] dynamics on [[D-branes]] with constant [[B-field]] [[gauge potential|potential]] [[effective field theory|effectively]] yields [[noncommutative field theories]].

Something special happens to this picture when the [[B-field]] [[gauge potential|potential]] $B$ has nonvanishing temporal components $B_{0i}$ (corresponding to *[[electric field|electric]]* [[flux density]] of the [[Chan-Paton gauge field]] on the [[D-brane]]): In this case there is a limiting critical field value where [[closed string]] interactions and hence [[gravity]] decouples and one is left (not just with a [[noncommutative QFT|noncommutative]] [[effective field theory]] but) with a pure [[open string|open]] [[string theory]] on [[noncommutative space]]. 

This decoupling limit is hence called *noncommutative open string theory* (NCOS).

At least for open strings on [[D4-branes]], this NCOS has a lift to [[M-theory]], namely to open [[membranes]] (OM) [[brane intersection|ending on]] [[M5-branes]] with critical [[self-dual higher gauge field|self-dual]] 3-form [[flux density]]. Here the corresponding decoupling limit is hence known as *OM theory*.

Incidentally, in this limit the angle vanishes between the [[M2-brane]] and the [[M5-brane]] that it intersects ([Michishita 2009](#Michishita2009)), making the M2-brane lie entirely inside the M5 (not just its boundary [[M-string]]).
