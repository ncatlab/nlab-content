
find an effective action for the electrons in the FQH system, subject to the external EM field $A$.

first, the electron current vector $\vec J$ couples via the current density 2-form $J = \iota_{\vec V} dvol$ to the external field by a term

$$
  A \wedge J
$$

where $\mathrm{d}J = 0$. The whole discussion being only local, this means that $J = \mathrm{d}a$, and hence the coupling term is 

$$
  A \wedge \mathrm{d}a
$$

With $a$ thus found to be a dynamical field, we ask for further interaction terms it may have, and the only gauge invariant one is the Chern-Simons action, hence

$$
  L 
  \,\coloneqq\,
  k \tfrac{1}{2}\, a\wedge \mathrm{d}a
  -
  A \wedge 
  \underset{J}{
    \underbrace{
      \mathrm{d}a
    }
  }
$$

The equation of motion is then

$$
  J = f \,=\, \tfrac{1}{k} F
$$

solved by

$$
  a \,=\, \tfrac{1}{k} A
$$

Hence on-shell the Lagrangian density is

$$
  L_{shell} 
  \;=\;
  \tfrac{1}{k}
  \tfrac{1}{2}
  A \wedge \mathrm{d}A
  \,.
$$

The first success of this model is that it knows about the correct Hall current, namely 

$$
  J_{shell}
  \;=\;
  \frac{
    \delta L_{shell}
  }{
    \delta A
  }
  \;=\;
  \tfrac{1}{k} \, F
  \,.
$$

(This follows already from the EOM itself, since $J = f$).

But this is the right effect, because it means that an electric field in $x$-direction $F = F_{0 1} \mathrm{d}t \wedge \mathrm{d}x$ induces (Hall) current $\vec J = \partial_y$ in the $y$-direction.

Finally we add a source term also for $a$, thought of as the current of quasi-holes:

$$
  L 
  \,\coloneqq\,
  k \tfrac{1}{2}\, a\wedge \mathrm{d}a
  \,-\,
  A \wedge 
  \underset{J}{
    \underbrace{
      \mathrm{d}a
    }
  }
  \,-\,
  a \wedge j
  \,.
$$

This changes the equation of motion to

$$
  J = f 
    \,=\,
  \tfrac{1}{k}\Big(
    F - j
  \Big)
$$

saying that the quasi-holes have $\tfrac{1}{k}$-fractional charge.
