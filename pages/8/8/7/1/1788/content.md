## Idea

In homological algebra one usually studies functors of the form $\mathcal{A}\to Ab, k-mod, \dots$ defined via derived functors where $\mathcal{A}$ is some category of groups, algebras over a ring $k$ and etc., and $Ab, k-mod$ are categories of abelian groups and $k$-modules, where $k$ is some ring. 


For instance, for the category of groups $\mathcal{A}=Gr$, using the left derived functors $L_q(\mathbb{Z}\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G}(-))$ of the functor of coinvariants $\mathbb{Z}\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G}(-): \mathbb{Z}G-mod\to Ab$ one usually defines group homology $H_q(G;A)=L_q(\mathbb{Z}\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G}(-))(A)$ of a group $G$ with coefficients in a $\mathbb{Z}G$-modules $A$.
