### Properties ###

<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[metrisable topological space|metrisable]]:
</summary>
  the topology is induced by a metric.
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[second-countable space|second-countable]]: 
</summary>
  there is a [[countable set|countable]] [[topological base|base]] of the topology.
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  $\sigma$-locally discrete base:  
</summary>
  the topology of $X$ is generated by a [[countably locally discrete set of subsets|$\sigma$-locally discrete]] [[topological base|base]].
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  $\sigma$-locally finite base: 
</summary>
  the topology of $X$ is generated by a [[countably locally finite set of subsets|countably locally finite]] [[topological base|base]].
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[separable space|separable]]:
</summary>
  there is a countable [[dense subspace|dense]]  subset.
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[Lindelöf topological space|Lindelöf]]: 
</summary>
  every [[open cover]] has a [[countable cover|countable]] sub-cover.
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[weakly Lindelöf topological space|weakly Lindelöf]]: 
</summary>
  every [[open cover]] has a [[countable set|countable]] subcollection the union of which is dense.
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[countable choice]]:
</summary>
  the [[natural number]]s is a [[projective object]] in [[Set]].
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[metacompact space|metacompact]]: 
</summary>
  every open cover has a point-finite open refinement.
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[first-countable space|first-countable]]: 
</summary>
  every point has a countable [[neighborhood base]]
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[Frechet-Uryson space]]: 
</summary>
  the closure of a set $A$ consists precisely of all limit points of sequences in $A$
</details>

<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[sequential topological space]]: 
</summary>
  a set $A$ is closed if it contains all limit points of sequences in $A$
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  [[countably tight space|countably tight]]: 
</summary>
  for each subset $A$ and each point $x\in \overline A$ there is a countable subset $D\subseteq A$ such that $x\in \overline D$.
</details>
</p>

### Implications ###

* [[metrisable spaces have countably locally discrete bases|a metric space has a $\sigma$-locally discrete base]]
  
* [[Nagata-Smirnov metrization theorem]]

<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  a second-countable space has a [[countably locally finite set of subsets|$\sigma$-locally finite]] [[topological base|base]]: 
</summary>
  take the the collection of singeltons of all elements of countable cover of $X$. 
</details>
<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  second-countable spaces are separable: 
</summary>
  use the axiom of [[countable choice]] to choose a point in each set of countable cover.
</details>

* [[second-countable spaces are Lindelöf]].

* [[weakly Lindelöf spaces with countably locally finite base are second countable]].

* [[separable metacompact spaces are Lindelöf]].

<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  separable spaces are weakly Lindelöf: 
</summary>
  given a countable dense subset and an open cover [[axiom of choice|choose]] for each point of the subset an open from the cover.
</details>

* Lindelöf spaces are trivially also weakly Lindelöf.

<details style="padding-left: 2em;">
<summary style="margin-left: -1em;">
  a space with a $\sigma$-locally finite base is first countable: 
</summary>
  obviously, every point is contained in at most countably many sets of a $\sigma$-locally finite base.
</details>

* a first-countable space is obviously Fréchet-Urysohn.

* a Fréchet-Uryson space is obviously sequential.

* a sequential space is obviously countably tight.
