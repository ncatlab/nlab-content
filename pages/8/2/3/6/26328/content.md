## Idea

Similar to how the [[interval type]] is the abstract [[homotopy type]] for the [[unit interval]] and the [[circle type]] is the abstract [[homotopy type]] for the [[unit circle]], the homotopical real numbers type is the abstract [[homotopy type]] for the [[real numbers]]. 

## Definition

The **homotopical real numbers type** $R$ is the [[higher inductive type]] generated by 

* an element $0:R$

* an [[equivalence of types]] $s:R \simeq R$

* for each $x:R$, an identification $p(x):x =_R s(x)$

Equivalently, it is the higher inductive type generated by 

* a function $c:\mathbb{Z} \to R$

* for each $z:\mathbb{Z}$, an identification $p(z):c(z) =_R c(s(z))$

where $\mathbb{Z}$ is the [[integers type]].

## Properties

### Relation to the integers

The homotopical real numbers type is the [[propositional truncation]] of the [[integers type]]. 

### Relation to the circle type

The [[circle type]] $S^1$ is the [[coequalizer type]] of the pair of functions on the homotopical reals

$$
R\underoverset{\quad s \quad}{\mathrm{id}_R}{\rightrightarrows}R \to S^1
$$

This is the analogue in [[synthetic homotopy theory]] of the fact that the unit circle is the coequalizer of the pair of functions on the real numbers in [[classical homotopy theory]]:

$$
\mathbb{R} \underoverset{\quad (-) + 1 \quad}{\mathrm{id}_\mathbb{R}}{\rightrightarrows}\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{S}^1
$$

### Relation to the real numbers

In [[real-cohesive homotopy type theory]], the [[shape]] of the [[real numbers]] is equivalent to the homotopical real numbers $\esh(\mathbb{R}) \simeq R$.
