## Idea

The (strong) _Whitney embedding theorem_ states that every [[smooth manifold]] ([[Hausdorff space|Hausdorff]] and [[sigma-compact]]) of [[dimension]] $n$ admits an [[embedding of smooth manifolds]] into the [[Euclidean space]] of dimension $2n$.

It is easier to see that every smooth manifold embeds into the Euclidean space of _some_ dimension ([this prop.](embedding+of+smooth+manifolds#ManifoldEmbedsIntoLargeDimensionalEuclideanSpace)). The force of Whitney's strong embedding theorem is to find the lowest dimension that still works in general. 

## Related concepts
