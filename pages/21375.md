## Idea

In [[nuclear physics]], the _quark-meson coupling model_ is an [[effective field theory]] of [[nuclei]] where effective [[meson]]-[[field (physics)|fields]] are [[interaction|coupled]] to [[quark]] [[field (physics)|fields]]. 

This is on contrast to [[quantum hadrodynamics]] of the form of [[baryon chiral perturbation theory]], such as the [[Walecka model]], where the effective [[fermion]] fields are instead the [[baryons]] (i.e. the [[confinement|confined]] [[bound states]] of three [[constituent quarks]]) with no isolated [[quarks]] appearing explicitly.
