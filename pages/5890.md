## Statement

Throughout, let $\mathcal{H}$ be a [[separable Hilbert space|separable]] infinite-dimensional [[complex numbers|complex]] [[Hilbert space]].

\begin{theorem}\label{KuiperTheorem}
**(Kuiper's theorem)**
\linebreak
The [[topological group|topological]] [[unitary group]] [[U(ℋ)|$\mathrm{U}(\mathcal{H})$]] in either the

* [norm topology](UH#NormTopology) 

or the equivalent

* [strong operator topology](UH#StrongOperatorTopology)

* [weak operator topology](UH#WeakOperatorTopology)

* [compact-open topology](UH#CompactOpenTopology)

is [[contractible]] in that there is a [[left homotopy]] between the [[identity]] $id \;\colon\; U(\mathcal{H}) \to U(\mathcal{H})$  and the [[constant function]] $const_{\mathrm{e}} \;\colon\; U(\mathcal{H}) \to U(\mathcal{H})$.

Similarly, since the [[general linear group]] $GL(\mathcal{H})$ of [[bounded operators]] is homotopy-equivalent to [[U(ℋ)|$\mathrm{U}(\mathcal{H})$]]  by the [[Gram-Schmidt process]], it, too, is [[contractible]].
\end{theorem}

See the commented list of references [below](#References).
