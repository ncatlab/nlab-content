## Idea 

A classical theorem of [[Jean Giraud|Giraud]] characterizes [[sheaf toposes]] abstractly as [[categories]] with certain [[properties]] known as *Giraud's axioms*

In [[higher topos theory]] there are corresponding [[higher category theory|higher]] analogs .

## Statement

\begin{proposition}
**(Giraud's theorem)**
\linebreak
A [[category]] is a [[Grothendieck topos]] if and only if it satisfies the following conditions:

1. it is [[locally presentable category|locally presentable]];

1. it has [[universal colimits]];

1. it has [[disjoint coproducts]];

1. it has [[effective quotients]].

\end{proposition}
(e.g. [Lurie 2009, Prop. 6.1.0.1](#Lurie09));

Similarly:

\begin{proposition}
**(Giraud-Rezk-Lurie theorem)**
\linebreak
An [[(infinity,1)-category|$(\infty,1)$-category]] is an [[(infinity,1)-category of (infinity,1)-sheaves|Grothendieck-Rezk-Lurie]]-[[(infinity,1)-topos]] if and only if it satisfies the following conditions:

1. it is [[locally presentable (infinity,1)-category|locally presentable]];

1. it has [[universal colimits|universal]] [[(infinity,1)-colimits|$\infty$-colimits]];

1. it has [[disjoint coproducts]];

1. it has [[groupoid objects in an (âˆž,1)-topos are effective|effective groupoid objects]].

\end{proposition}
(e.g. [Lurie 2009, Prop. 6.1.0.6](#Lurie09))

\linebreak

Similarly, [[2-category theory]] there is the analogous *[[2-Giraud theorem]]* for [[2-toposes]].
