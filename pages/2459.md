Let $G$ be a discrete [[group]], $k$ a [[commutative unital ring]], $k[G]$ the [[group ring]] of $G$ and $M$ a left $k[G]$-[[module]]. Then there is well-defined $k$-module $M_G = M/\langle{m - g m}\rangle$ called the module of __$G$-coinvariants__. Here $\langle{m - g m}\rangle$ denotes the smallest sub-$k$-module of $M$ containing all expressions of the form $m - g m$ where $g\in G$ and $m\in M$. 

Let $C$ be a $k$-[[coalgebra]], $\chi$ a [[group-like element]], that is an element such that $\Delta_C(\chi)=\chi\otimes\chi$, and $\rho:V\to V\otimes C$ a right $C$-[[coaction]]. Any element $v\in V$ such that $\rho(v)=v\otimes\chi$ is called a __$(\rho,\chi)$-coinvariant element__ in the $C$-cmodule $(V,\rho)$. Suppose $H$ is a [[bialgebra]], $A$ an algebra and $\rho:A\to A\otimes H$ a coaction making $A$ into a right $H$-comodule algebra. The unit element $1_H$ is a group-like element, and we call $(\rho,1)$-coinvariants simply __$\rho$-coinvariants__. The subset of $\rho$-coinvariants in $A$ is a subalgebra, called the __subalgebra of coinvariants__.
