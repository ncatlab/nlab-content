## Idea

A _Kähler polarization_ of a [[symplectic manifold]] is a [[polarization]] of induced by a compatible [[Kähler manifold]] structure.

$$
  \mathcal{P} \subset T_{\mathbb{C}} T X
$$

$$
  \mathcal{P} \cap \overline{\mathcal{P}} = 0
$$

$$
  \mathcal{P} = \left\{J v - i v \;|\; v \in T X \right\}
$$

Given a [[prequantization]] of this by a [[holomorphic line bundle]], then the polarized sections are the _holomorphic sections_.

Hence the concept of Kähler polarization is that special case of _[[polarization]]_ which connects most intimately the [[symplectic geometry]] to [[complex analytic geometry]]. The generalization of this from [[complex analytic geometry]] to more general [[algebraic geometry]] is the concept of a _[[polarized algebraic variety]]_.
