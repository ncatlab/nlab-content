## Idea

[[Algebraic cobordism]] is the algebraic or [[motives|motivic]] analogue of [[complex cobordism]].  It was constructed by [[Vladimir Voevodsky]] as the [[cohomology theory]] represented by a certain [[motivic spectrum]] $MGL$, called the algebraic cobordism spectrum.  A more geometric description, over fields of characteristic zero, was given by [[Marc Levine]] and [[Fabien Morel]].  An even simpler description was later given by [[Marc Levine]] and [[Rahul Pandharipande]].  (An earlier construction is due to work of [[Victor Snaith]] in the late 1970's.)

### As the universal oriented Borel-Moore homology theory

The notion of **oriented Borel-Moore homology** is a generalization of the theory $CH_* : Sch_K \to AbGrp_*$ of [[Chow groups]] on [[schemes]] over a [[field]] $K$.  (Here 'oriented' means the existence of [[proper]] [[direct image]] homomorphisms.)  The additivity of the first [[Chern class]] operator (the formula $c_1(L \otimes M) = c_1(L) + c_1(M)$), is replaced by imposing a [[formal group law]] $F$, i.e. $c_1(L \otimes M) = F(c_1(L), c_1(M))$.  Algebraic cobordism is the universal oriented Borel-Moore homology theory.
