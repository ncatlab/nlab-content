Team logic and team semantics

In team semantics the truth of a propositional formula is considered in a set of valuations, called a team, rather than in an individual valuation. 

## Literature

Some early ideas are from

* W. Hodges, _Compositional semantics for a language of imperfect information_, Log. J. IGPL 5, 539–563 (1997)

The framework is explicitly developed by J. Väänänen as an extension to [[dependence logic]] by classical negation.

* Jouko Väänänen, Interactive logic. Selected papers from the 7th Augustus de Morgan workshop, 281-302, Amsterdam University Press 2007
* Fan Yang, Jouko Väänänen, _Propositional team logics_,
Annals of Pure and Applied Logic __168__:7 (2017) 1406-1441 [doi](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apal.2017.01.007)
* J. Kontinen, J. Väänänen, _On definability in dependence logic_, Journal of Logic, Language and Information, 18(3) (2009) 317–332.
* Juha Kontinen, Ville Nurmi, _Team logic and second-order logic_, Fundamenta Informaticae 106, no. 2-4, 259-272, 2011, [doi:10.3233/FI-2011-386](https://doi.org/10.3233/FI-2011-386)
