
Given an [[object]] $X$ in a [[category]] $B$ the [[domain cofibration|domain functor]] $(Y\stackrel{f}\to X)\mapsto Y$ from the [[slice category]] $B/X$ to $B$ is a [[fibered category]] (i.e. Grothendieck fibration). 

Any fibered category isomorphic to the $dom:B/X\to B$ is said to be __representable__. This is because under the [[Grothendieck construction]] representable fibered categories correspond precisely to [[representable functor]]s $B^{op} \to Set \hookrightarrow Cat$: the category $B/X$ is the [[category of elements]] of the representable functor $B(-,X)$.
